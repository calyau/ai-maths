# Quick Definition

The alternating group $A_n$ is generated by cycles of length three.

# Core Definition

"The alternating group $A_n$ is generated by cycles of length three" (Milne, Corollary 4.28, p. 66).

# Prerequisites

- **Alternating group** — The group being generated
- **Transposition** — The proof uses that any even permutation is a product of pairs of transpositions

# Key Properties

1. Any $\sigma \in A_n$ is a product of an even number of transpositions $t_1 t_1' \cdots t_m t_m'$
2. A product of two transpositions is a product of 3-cycles:
   - $(ij)(jl) = (ijl)$
   - $(ij)(kl) = (ijk)(jkl)$ when $i,j,k,l$ distinct
   - $(ij)(ij) = 1$
3. This is used in the proof that $A_n$ is simple for $n \geq 5$

# Context & Application

This generation result is the starting point for proving that $A_n$ is simple: any normal subgroup containing a 3-cycle must contain all 3-cycles, hence all of $A_n$.
