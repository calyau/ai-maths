# Quick Definition

The Holder program is the strategy of classifying all finite groups by (A) classifying all finite simple groups, and (B) classifying all extensions of finite groups.

# Core Definition

"Every finite group can be obtained by taking repeated extensions of simple groups. Thus the simple finite groups can be regarded as the basic building blocks for all finite groups. The problem of classifying all simple groups falls into two parts: A. Classify all finite simple groups; B. Classify all extensions of finite groups" (Milne, p. 51).

# Prerequisites

- **Simple group** — The building blocks in the program
- **Extension of groups** — The mechanism for combining simple groups

# Key Properties

1. Every finite group has a composition series with simple factors
2. Part A (classification of simple groups) is complete
3. Part B (classifying extensions) is far from complete
4. "The classification of all finite groups is completely infeasible" (Solomon 2001, quoted p. 52)

# Context & Application

The Holder program, originating with Otto Holder (1892), provides the conceptual framework for understanding finite groups. While Part A was completed (announced 1982, confirmed with Aschbacher-Smith 2004), Part B remains intractable in general.
