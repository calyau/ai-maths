# Quick Definition

The Brauer group Br(F) of a field F is the group of isomorphism classes of central division algebras over F, with multiplication given by tensor product.

# Core Definition

Brauer showed that the set of isomorphism classes of central division algebras over F can be made into a group, now called the **Brauer group** of F. The product is [D][D'] = [D''] where D tensor D' is isomorphic to M_r(D''). The identity is [F], and the inverse of [D] is [D^opp]. (Milne, Chapter 7, p. 111)

# Prerequisites

- **Division algebra** — elements of the Brauer group
- **Simple F-algebra** — central simple algebras provide the context

# Key Properties

1. Br(F) = 0 if F is algebraically closed (only division algebra is F)
2. Br(F) = 0 if F is finite (Wedderburn's theorem: finite division algebras are commutative)
3. Br(R) = Z/2Z (generated by the quaternions H(-1,-1))
4. The Brauer groups of Q and its extensions were computed by Albert, Brauer, Hasse, Noether (class field theory)

# Context & Application

The Brauer group measures the complexity of division algebras over F. For representation theory over algebraically closed fields, the Brauer group is trivial, which is why all simple algebras are matrix algebras over F.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 111): Br(R) has order 2, generated by [H(-1,-1)].

**Example 2** (p. 111): Br(F) = 0 for F algebraically closed or finite.
