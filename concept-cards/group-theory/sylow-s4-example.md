# Quick Definition

In S_4 (order 24 = 2^3 * 3), the Sylow 3-subgroups are the stabilizers of faces of a tetrahedron (4 subgroups, each isomorphic to C_3), and the Sylow 2-subgroups are the stabilizers of pairs of opposite edges (3 subgroups, each isomorphic to D_4).

# Core Definition

**Example 5.11.** The group S_4 has order 24 = 2^3 * 3. Visualizing S_4 through its action on a tetrahedron:
- The Sylow 3-subgroups are the stabilizers of faces (each is C_3 generated by a 3-cycle). They are conjugate because S_4 acts transitively on faces.
- The Sylow 2-subgroups are the stabilizers of pairs of opposite edges (each has order 8).

(Milne, Example 5.11, p. 80)

# Key Properties

1. s_3 = 4 (four faces of tetrahedron), confirming s_3 | 8 and s_3 congruent to 1 mod 3
2. s_2 = 3 (three pairs of opposite edges), confirming s_2 | 3 and s_2 congruent to 1 mod 2
3. The geometric picture makes conjugacy visually obvious

# Context & Application

This is a key example connecting abstract Sylow theory with concrete geometric symmetry. It shows how the combinatorics of s_p constraints match the geometry of the symmetric group acting on a polyhedron.
