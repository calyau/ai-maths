# Quick Definition

The general linear group GL_n(F) is the group of n x n invertible matrices with entries in a field F, under matrix multiplication. For a vector space V, GL(V) is the group of all linear automorphisms of V.

# Core Definition

The n x n matrices with coefficients in a field F and nonzero determinant form a group GL_n(F) called the **general linear group of degree n**. For a finite-dimensional F-vector space V, the F-linear automorphisms of V form a group GL(V) called the **general linear group of V**. The choice of a basis for V determines an isomorphism GL(V) -> GL_n(F) (Example 1.7, p. 9).

# Prerequisites

- **Group** — GL_n(F) is an example of a group

# Key Properties

1. Elements are n x n matrices with nonzero determinant (equivalently, invertible matrices)
2. The group operation is matrix multiplication
3. The neutral element is the identity matrix I_n
4. GL_n(F) is noncommutative for n >= 2
5. GL(V) ≈ GL_n(F) when dim(V) = n (after choosing a basis)

# Construction / Recognition

## To Construct:
1. Fix a field F and a positive integer n
2. Take all n x n matrices over F with nonzero determinant
3. The group operation is matrix multiplication

# Context & Application

The general linear group is one of the most important examples of a non-abelian group. It serves as the ambient group for many classical groups (orthogonal, symplectic, special linear). The quaternion group Q is realized as a subgroup of GL_2(C) (1.18, p. 13).

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 9, 1.7): GL_n(F) consists of all invertible n x n matrices over F.

**Example 2** (p. 13, 1.18): The quaternion group Q is a subgroup of GL_2(C), generated by specific 2 x 2 complex matrices.

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Including singular matrices (det = 0) in GL_n(F)
  **Correction**: GL_n(F) requires nonzero determinant (invertibility)

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Confusing GL_n(F) with M_n(F) (all n x n matrices)
  **Clarification**: M_n(F) is not a group under multiplication (singular matrices have no inverse); GL_n(F) is the invertible subset
