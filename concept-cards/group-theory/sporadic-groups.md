# Quick Definition

The 26 sporadic groups are finite simple groups that do not belong to any infinite family in the classification.

# Core Definition

The classification lists "the 26 'sporadic groups'" (Milne, p. 51) as class (d). "Some have even speculated that the largest of them, the Fischer-Griess monster, is built into the fabric of the universe" (p. 52).

# Prerequisites

- **Classification of finite simple groups** — Sporadic groups are class (d) in the list

# Key Properties

1. Exactly 26 sporadic groups exist
2. They do not fit into infinite parametric families
3. The largest is the Fischer-Griess monster group
4. Despite their small count, they are of great mathematical interest
