# Quick Definition

The monster group is the largest of the 26 sporadic simple groups.

# Core Definition

"Some have even speculated that the largest of them, the Fischer-Griess monster, is built into the fabric of the universe" (Milne, p. 52). It is the largest sporadic finite simple group.

# Prerequisites

- **Sporadic groups** — The monster is the largest sporadic group

# Key Properties

1. Largest sporadic simple group
2. Also known as the Fischer-Griess monster
3. Subject of the "monstrous moonshine" conjecture connecting it to modular functions
