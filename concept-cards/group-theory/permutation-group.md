# Quick Definition

A permutation group is a subgroup of $\operatorname{Sym}(X)$ for some set $X$; equivalently, a group of bijections $X \to X$ under composition.

# Core Definition

The symmetric group $S_n = \operatorname{Sym}(\{1, 2, \ldots, n\})$ is the group of all permutations of $n$ elements (Milne, p. 63). Any subgroup of $S_n$ is a permutation group. By Cayley's theorem, every group is isomorphic to a permutation group.

# Prerequisites

- **Group action** — A permutation group is a group acting faithfully
- **Faithful action** — The action realizes the group as a subgroup of $\operatorname{Sym}(X)$

# Key Properties

1. $|S_n| = n!$
2. $S_n$ is generated by transpositions
3. Every permutation has a unique decomposition into disjoint cycles (Proposition 4.26)
4. The order of a permutation is the lcm of its cycle lengths

# Context & Application

Permutation groups are historically the first groups studied and remain central to group theory. Every finite group embeds in some $S_n$.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 63): The permutation $\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 & 7 \\ 2 & 5 & 7 & 4 & 3 & 1 & 6 \end{pmatrix}$ sends $1 \mapsto 2$, $2 \mapsto 5$, etc.
