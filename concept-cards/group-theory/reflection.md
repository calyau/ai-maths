# Quick Definition

A reflection on $V = \mathbb{R}^n$ (with a positive definite symmetric bilinear form) is a linear map $s_\alpha$ that sends a nonzero vector $\alpha$ to $-\alpha$ and fixes the hyperplane $H_\alpha$ orthogonal to $\alpha$.

# Core Definition

"A *reflection* is a linear map $s: V \to V$ sending some nonzero vector $\alpha$ to $-\alpha$ and fixing the points of the hyperplane $H_\alpha$ orthogonal to $\alpha$. We write $s_\alpha$ for the reflection defined by $\alpha$; it is given by the formula $s_\alpha v = v - \frac{2(v, \alpha)}{(\alpha, \alpha)}\alpha$." (Milne, 2.14, p. 39)

# Prerequisites

- **Group** — reflections are group elements (involutions)

# Key Properties

1. $s_\alpha$ has order 2: $s_\alpha^2 = \mathrm{id}$
2. Formula: $s_\alpha(v) = v - \frac{2(v, \alpha)}{(\alpha, \alpha)}\alpha$
3. $s_\alpha$ fixes the hyperplane $H_\alpha = \{v \mid (v, \alpha) = 0\}$
4. $s_\alpha$ preserves the inner product: $(s_\alpha v, s_\alpha w) = (v, w)$
5. The composite of two reflections whose fixed hyperplanes meet at angle $\pi/n$ is a rotation through $2\pi/n$

# Context & Application

Reflections are the building blocks of Coxeter groups. Every finite Coxeter group can be realized as a group generated by reflections in $\mathbb{R}^n$.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 39): Transpositions $(ij)$ in $S_n$ act as reflections on $\mathbb{R}^n$, sending $e_i - e_j$ to its negative and fixing the hyperplane $\{x_i = x_j\}$.

**Example 2** (p. 39): In $D_n$, the composite of two reflections through adjacent diagonals (meeting at angle $\pi/n$) is a rotation through $2\pi/n$.
