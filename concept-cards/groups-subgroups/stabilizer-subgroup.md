# Quick Definition

The stabilizer G_W of a subspace W in a representation G -> GL_V is the subgroup functor R -> {g in G(R) | gW_R = W_R}. It is a closed subgroup of G, represented by a quotient of O(G).

# Core Definition

Let G -> GL_V be a representation and W a subspace of V. The *stabilizer* of W in G is the functor G_W: R -> {g in G(R) | gW_R = W_R}. Choosing a basis (e_i) for W, extending to a basis of V, and writing rho(e_j) = sum e_i tensor a_{ij}, the stabilizer G_W is represented by the quotient of O(G) by the ideal generated by {a_{ij} | j in J, i in I} where J indexes basis elements of W and I indexes the complementary basis elements (Milne, Proposition 8.52, pp. 111-112). The *isotropy group* G_v of a vector v is the subgroup fixing v: G_v(R) = {g in G(R) | g(v tensor 1) = v tensor 1} (Proposition 8.55).

# Key Properties

1. G_W is a closed subgroup of G (Proposition 8.52)
2. The stabilizer of W tensor W' in V tensor V' is G_W intersect G_{W'} (Proposition 8.54)
3. The space V^G = {v in V | rho(v) = v tensor 1} is the largest trivial subrepresentation
4. If H(k) is dense in H and hW = W for all h in H(k), then H stabilizes W (Corollary 8.53)

# Context & Application

Stabilizers connect representation theory with the internal structure of algebraic groups. Chevalley's theorem (8.57) shows that every subgroup of an algebraic group is the stabilizer of a one-dimensional subspace in some representation.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 112): The Borel subgroup B of GL_n is the stabilizer of the standard flag, i.e., the stabilizer of the chain of subspaces <e_1> subset <e_1, e_2> subset ... subset <e_1,...,e_{n-1}>.
