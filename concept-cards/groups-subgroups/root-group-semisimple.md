# Quick Definition

For a split semisimple algebraic group (G,T) in characteristic zero, the root group U_α is the image of the unipotent radical 𝕌₂ of SL₂ under the homomorphism φ: SL₂ → G determined by a root α and an sl₂-triple.

# Core Definition

For each root α of (G,T) and choice of nonzero element in 𝔤^α, there is a unique homomorphism φ: SL₂ → G such that Lie(φ) is the inclusion 𝔰_α → 𝔤 (Milne, Proposition 3.22, p. 325). The **root group** U_α is the image of 𝕌₂ under φ. It is isomorphic to 𝔾_a and satisfies t · u_α(a) · t⁻¹ = u_α(α(t)a).

# Prerequisites

- **Split semisimple algebraic group** — Root groups exist in this context
- **sl₂-triple** — Determines the homomorphism SL₂ → G

# Key Properties

1. U_α ≅ 𝔾_a
2. Uniquely determined by α (independent of the choice of nonzero element in 𝔤^α)
3. The Borel subgroup is generated by T and all U_α for α ∈ R⁺
