# Quick Definition

The rank of a reductive group is the dimension of a maximal torus, i.e., the largest r such that G_{k^{al}} contains a subgroup isomorphic to 𝔾_m^r. It equals the rank of the root datum.

# Core Definition

The **rank** of a reductive group is the dimension of a maximal torus, i.e., the largest r such that G_{k^{al}} contains a subgroup isomorphic to 𝔾_m^r. Since all maximal tori are conjugate, the rank is well-defined (Milne, p. 342).

# Prerequisites

- **Reductive algebraic group** — Rank is defined for reductive groups
- **Split maximal torus** — Rank = dim T

# Key Properties

1. Semisimple group of rank 0 is trivial (Theorem 2.6a)
2. Semisimple group of rank 1 over k^{al} is isomorphic to SL₂ or PGL₂ (Theorem 2.6b)
3. Rank of root datum = rank of V = dim T for semisimple; = rank X for general reductive
