# Quick Definition

The Dieudonne module M(G) of a commutative finite affine p-group G over a perfect field k is a W(k)-module of finite length equipped with Frobenius F and Verschiebung V satisfying FV = VF = p. This provides a contravariant equivalence from finite commutative p-groups to semi-linear algebra.

# Core Definition

*Theorem 12.15*: Over a perfect field k of characteristic p, there exists a contravariant equivalence G -> M(G) from commutative finite affine p-groups to triples (M, F, V) where M is a W(k)-module of finite length (W = Witt vectors) and F, V are endomorphisms satisfying F(cm) = sigma(c)Fm, V(sigma(c)m) = cVm, and FV = p.id = VF (sigma = Frobenius of W). The order of G is p^{length(M(G))} (Milne, pp. 149-150).

- **Finite flat affine group** -- Dieudonne modules classify finite commutative p-groups

# Key Properties

1. Contravariant equivalence to semi-linear algebra (Theorem 12.15)
2. Order of G = p^{length(M(G))}
3. Commutes with perfect base change: M(G_{k'}) = W(k') tensor_{W(k)} M(G)
4. M(Z/pZ) = W/pW with F=1, V=0; M(mu_p) = W/pW with F=0, V=p; M(alpha_p) = W/pW with F=0, V=0

# Context & Application

Dieudonne modules reduce the classification of commutative finite p-groups over perfect fields to linear algebra (over the non-commutative ring generated by F and V). This is fundamental in the theory of p-divisible groups and abelian varieties in positive characteristic.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 150): M(Z/pZ) = W/pW with F = identity, V = 0 (etale group).

**Example 2** (p. 150): M(mu_p) = W/pW with F = 0, V = multiplication by p (connected multiplicative group).

**Example 3** (p. 150): M(alpha_p) = W/pW with F = 0, V = 0 (connected, neither etale nor multiplicative).
