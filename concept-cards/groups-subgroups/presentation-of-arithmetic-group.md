# Quick Definition

Every arithmetic subgroup of an algebraic group over $\mathbb{Q}$ is finitely generated and finitely presented. For $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})/\{\pm I\}$, the presentation is $\langle S, T \mid S^2, (ST)^3\rangle$.

# Core Definition

Using reduction theory, one shows that for an arithmetic subgroup $\Gamma$ of $G(\mathbb{Q})$, the set $\{\gamma \in \Gamma \mid \gamma D \cap D \neq \emptyset\}$ is finite (where $D = \bigcup_{g \in C} g\mathfrak{S}/K$), and this finite set generates $\Gamma$. Moreover, $\Gamma$ has a finite presentation (Milne, p. 408).

For $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})/\{\pm I\}$, the group is generated by $S = \begin{pmatrix}0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0\end{pmatrix}$ and $T = \begin{pmatrix}1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1\end{pmatrix}$ (Proposition 13.1), with presentation $\langle S, T \mid S^2, (ST)^3\rangle$.

# Key Properties

1. Arithmetic subgroups are finitely generated
2. Arithmetic subgroups are finitely presented
3. $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})/\{\pm I\} = \langle S, T \mid S^2, (ST)^3\rangle$
4. Every torsion-free subgroup $\Gamma$ of $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ is free on $1 + \frac{(\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}):\Gamma)}{12}$ generators
5. The commutator subgroup of $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ has index 12 and is free on 2 generators

# Construction / Recognition

## To Find Generators:
1. Compute a Siegel set $\mathfrak{S}$ and finite set $C$ with $G(\mathbb{R}) = \Gamma \cdot C \cdot \mathfrak{S}$
2. Form $D = \bigcup_{g \in C} g\mathfrak{S}/K$
3. The finite set $\{\gamma \in \Gamma \mid \gamma D \cap D \neq \emptyset\}$ generates $\Gamma$

# Context & Application

The finite presentability of arithmetic groups is a fundamental structural result that follows from reduction theory. It connects the algebraic theory of arithmetic groups to combinatorial group theory.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 408): $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})/\{\pm I\}$ has presentation $\langle S, T \mid S^2, (ST)^3\rangle$ where $S(z) = -1/z$ and $T(z) = z + 1$.

**Example 2** (p. 408): The commutator subgroup of $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ has index 12 and is free on generators $\begin{pmatrix}1 & 1 \\ 1 & 2\end{pmatrix}$ and $\begin{pmatrix}2 & 1 \\ 1 & 1\end{pmatrix}$ (noting the text states it is free on 2 generators since $1 + 12/12 = 2$).

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Expecting a torsion-free subgroup of $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ to require fewer generators than $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$
  **Correction**: Torsion-free subgroups of $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ are free and can require more generators than the group itself

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Thinking $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ is a free group
  **Clarification**: $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})/\{\pm I\}$ is a free product $\mathbb{Z}/2 * \mathbb{Z}/3$, not a free group; torsion-free subgroups are free
