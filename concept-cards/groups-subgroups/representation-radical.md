# Quick Definition

The representation radical N(G) of a complex Lie group G is the intersection of the kernels of all simple representations. It is the largest closed normal subgroup acting unipotently on every representation.

# Core Definition

For a complex Lie group G, the **representation radical** N(G) is the intersection of the kernels of all simple representations of G. It is the largest closed normal subgroup of G whose action on every representation is unipotent. When G is linear, N(G) is the radical of the derived group of G (Milne, p. 330).

# Prerequisites

- **Complex Lie group** — N(G) is defined for complex Lie groups

# Key Properties

1. N(G) acts unipotently on every representation
2. When G is linear, N(G) = Rad(𝒟G)
3. Rep_ℂ(G) is algebraic iff G = N(G) ⋊ (reductive subgroup) (Theorem 2.9)
