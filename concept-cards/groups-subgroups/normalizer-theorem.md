# Quick Definition

The normalizer theorem states that for any Borel subgroup B of a connected algebraic group G, N_G(B) = B. A Borel subgroup is its own normalizer.

# Core Definition

**Theorem 3.42 (Normalizer Theorem)**: For any Borel subgroup B of a connected reductive group G, N_G(B) = B (Milne, p. 363). This follows from the Bruhat decomposition and the simple transitivity of W on Weyl chambers.

# Prerequisites

- **Borel subgroup** — B is a Borel subgroup
- **Bruhat decomposition** — Used in the proof

# Key Properties

1. Immediate corollary: W = N_G(T)/C_G(T) (Corollary 3.43)
2. Implies Borel subgroups are determined by their normalizers
