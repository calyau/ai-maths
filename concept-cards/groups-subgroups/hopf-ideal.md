# Quick Definition

A Hopf ideal in a Hopf algebra A is an ideal a that is also a coideal (Delta(a) subset of a tensor A + A tensor a and epsilon(a) = 0) and is stable under the antipode (S(a) subset of a). Hopf ideals correspond bijectively to affine subgroups.

# Core Definition

A k-subspace a of a k-bialgebra A is a **bi-ideal** if it is both an ideal and a co-ideal, i.e., Delta(a) is a subset of a tensor A + A tensor a and epsilon(a) = 0. When A admits an inversion S, a bi-ideal a is a **Hopf ideal** if additionally S(a) is a subset of a (Exercise 5-10, p. 51).

The quotient A/a inherits a unique bialgebra (resp. Hopf algebra) structure making A -> A/a a homomorphism, and any homomorphism whose kernel contains a factors uniquely through A -> A/a (Exercise 5-10b).

# Prerequisites

- **Hopf algebra** — Hopf ideals are defined in Hopf algebras

# Key Properties

1. The kernel of any Hopf algebra homomorphism is a Hopf ideal (Exercise 5-10a)
2. The image of any Hopf algebra homomorphism is a Hopf algebra (Exercise 5-10a)
3. A/Ker(f) is isomorphic to Im(f) for any Hopf algebra homomorphism f (Exercise 5-10c)
4. Hopf ideals of O(G) are in bijection with affine subgroups of G (Proposition 7.8, p. 74)

# Context & Application

Hopf ideals are the algebraic counterpart of affine subgroups. The bijection between Hopf ideals in O(G) and affine subgroups of G (Proposition 7.8) is the fundamental correspondence that makes the Hopf algebra approach effective for studying the group structure.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 76): The ideal (X^n - 1) in k[X, X^{-1}] = O(G_m) is the Hopf ideal corresponding to the subgroup mu_n.

**Example 2** (p. 77): The ideal generated by det(X_ij) - 1 in O(GL_n) is the Hopf ideal corresponding to SL_n.
