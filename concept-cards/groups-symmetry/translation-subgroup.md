# Quick Definition

The translation subgroup H of a subgroup G of E_2 is the intersection G intersect T, consisting of all translations in G. For a wallpaper group, H is generated by two independent translations and is always a normal subgroup of G.

# Core Definition

"If G is a subgroup of E_2, we write H for G intersect T ... calling H the translation subgroup of G" (Armstrong, Ch. 25, p. 153). For a wallpaper group, H is generated by two independent translations. Since T is normal in E_2, H is normal in G, and the point group J is isomorphic to G/H by the First Isomorphism Theorem.

# Prerequisites

- **Wallpaper group** -- The translation subgroup is a key component of a wallpaper group
- **Euclidean group** -- T is the translation subgroup of E_2
- **Normal subgroup** -- H is normal in G because T is normal in E_2

# Key Properties

1. H = G intersect T, consisting of all translations in G
2. H is a normal subgroup of G
3. For a wallpaper group, H is generated by two linearly independent translations
4. The point group J is isomorphic to G/H
5. The orbit of the origin under H is the lattice L

# Construction / Recognition

## To Find the Translation Subgroup
1. Given a wallpaper group G with generators
2. Identify all elements of the form (v, I) -- these are the translations
3. Find two independent translations that generate all others
4. These generators span the lattice

# Context & Application

The translation subgroup captures the periodicity of a wallpaper pattern. Its orbit of the origin gives the lattice L, which is the key geometric object governing the classification. The quotient G/H equals the point group, separating the "periodic" and "rotational/reflective" components of the symmetry.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 153): For the unshaded hexagonal pattern, H is generated by translations tau_1 and tau_2 along the two independent lattice directions.

**Example 2** (p. 155): For the group generated by tau(x,y) = (x+1,y) and h(x,y) = (-x, y+1), the translation subgroup is generated by (1,0) and (0,2) -- note that h^2 gives the translation (0,2).

# Relationships

## Enables
- **Lattice** -- The lattice is the orbit of the origin under H
- **Point group** -- J = G/H

## Related
- **Point group** -- J = pi(G) is the "complement" measuring rotational/reflective symmetry

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Assuming all elements of G that "look translational" are in H
  **Correction**: Only pure translations (v, I) belong to H; glide reflections move points translationally but are not translations

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Thinking the lattice vectors are the same as the generators of G
  **Clarification**: G may be generated by glides or rotations whose squares or combinations give translations; the lattice is determined by the translations in G, not the generators
