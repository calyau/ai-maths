# Quick Definition

A $5 \times 1$ strip with a half-twist forms a Mobius band with 10 squares. With 3 colours and the symmetry group $D_{10}$ (order 20), there are 3210 distinct painted Mobius bands.

# Core Definition

Armstrong considers a Mobius band $M$ made from a $5 \times 1$ strip joined with a half twist, giving 10 unit squares. The symmetry group is $D_{10}$ (generated by a slide $r$ with $r^{10} = e$ and a flip $s$ with $s^2 = e$). Applying the Counting Theorem with 3 colours (Armstrong, pp. 107--108):

$$\frac{1}{20}\{3^{10} + 2 \cdot 3 + 2 \cdot 3^2 + 2 \cdot 3 + 2 \cdot 3^2 + 3^5 + 5 \cdot 3^6 + 5 \cdot 3^5\} = 3210$$

# Key Properties

1. The symmetry group is $D_{10}$, order 20
2. The Mobius band is described as a subset of $\mathbb{C} \times \mathbb{C}$: $\{(e^{2i\theta}, \lambda e^{i\theta}) \mid -\pi < \theta \le \pi, 0 \le \lambda \le 1\}$
3. $r$ sends $(e^{2i\theta}, \lambda e^{i\theta})$ to $(e^{2i(\theta+\pi/5)}, \lambda e^{i(\theta+\pi/5)})$
4. $s$ sends $(e^{2i\theta}, \lambda e^{i\theta})$ to $(e^{-2i\theta}, \lambda e^{-i\theta})$
5. The symmetry $r$ is not induced by a rotation of $\mathbb{R}^3$; it is a movement of the band in itself

# Context & Application

This example (suggested by L.M. Woodward) shows the Counting Theorem applied to a non-standard symmetry group arising from a topologically interesting object. The Mobius band's half-twist creates a symmetry that is not a spatial rotation.
