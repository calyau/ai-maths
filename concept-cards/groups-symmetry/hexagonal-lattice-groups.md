# Quick Definition

A hexagonal lattice admits five new wallpaper groups with 3-fold or 6-fold rotation: p3, p3m1, p31m, p6, and p6mm.

# Core Definition

"Case (e). The lattice of G is hexagonal. Then the point group must be contained in the dihedral group of order 12 generated by A_{pi/3} and B_0" (Armstrong, Ch. 26, p. 168).

**p3**: J generated by A_{2pi/3} (order-3 rotations only).

**p3m1**: J generated by A_{2pi/3} and B_0 (reflections in one set of directions).

**p31m**: J generated by A_{2pi/3} and B_{pi/3} (reflections in a different set of directions). The analysis shows that (0, B_{pi/3}) must belong to G, so reflections are present.

**p6**: J generated by A_{pi/3} (order-6 rotations).

**p6mm**: J generated by A_{pi/3} and B_0 (full dihedral symmetry D_6).

# Key Properties

1. p3: point group Z_3; only order-3 rotations, no reflections or glides
2. p3m1: point group D_3; reflections through lattice points
3. p31m: point group D_3; reflections that do NOT pass through all rotation centres
4. p3m1 and p31m are non-isomorphic despite having the same point group D_3 (Theorem 26.4)
5. In p31m, every order-3 rotation is a product of two reflections; in p3m1, some are not
6. p6: point group Z_6; has rotations of orders 2, 3, and 6
7. p6mm: point group D_6; full hexagonal dihedral symmetry

# Construction / Recognition

## p31m Analysis (Armstrong, pp. 168-169)
1. Choose fixed point of order-3 rotation as origin
2. B_{pi/3} is realized by (lambda*a + mu*b, B_{pi/3})
3. Squaring and combining with rotations forces lambda and mu to be integers
4. Therefore (0, B_{pi/3}) belongs to G -- reflections are present
5. Elements: (ma + nb, M) for integer m, n and M in {I, A_{2pi/3}, A_{4pi/3}, B_{pi/3}, B_pi, B_{5pi/3}}

## Distinguishing p3m1 from p31m (Theorem 26.4)
- In p31m: every order-3 rotation is the product of two reflections in the group
- In p3m1: the rotation (a, A_{2pi/3}) cannot be factored as a product of two reflections

# Context & Application

The hexagonal groups include some of the most visually striking wallpaper patterns. The honeycomb pattern of hexagons mentioned in Chapter 25 has the symmetry of p6mm. The distinction between p3m1 and p31m is notoriously subtle and depends on the relative orientation of mirror lines and rotation centres.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 169): In p31m, elements (ma + nb, B_pi) are reflections when n = 0 (vertical mirrors) and vertical glides when n is not 0.

**Example 2** (Ch. 25, p. 152): The unshaded hexagonal pattern has wallpaper group p6mm (point group D_6, hexagonal lattice).

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Confusing p3m1 and p31m by assuming the names are interchangeable
  **Correction**: They are genuinely different groups; the distinction lies in whether rotation centres sit on mirror lines

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Thinking the "1" in p31m is a placeholder with no meaning
  **Clarification**: The position of "1" indicates which symmetry direction has no mirror; it distinguishes the two D_3-point-group wallpaper groups
