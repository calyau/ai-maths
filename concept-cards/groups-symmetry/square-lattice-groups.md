# Quick Definition

A square lattice admits three new wallpaper groups with 4-fold rotation: p4 (rotations only), p4mm (rotations and reflections), and p4gm (rotations with glide reflections replacing some mirrors).

# Core Definition

"Case (d). The lattice of G is square. Then the group of orthogonal transformations which preserves L is the dihedral group of order 8 generated by A_{pi/2} and B_0" (Armstrong, Ch. 26, p. 167).

**p4**: J is generated by A_{pi/2} (quarter-turn rotations, no reflections).

**p4mm**: J is generated by A_{pi/2} and B_0, with B_0 realized by a reflection.

**p4gm**: J is generated by A_{pi/2} and B_0, but B_0 cannot be realized by a reflection. Contains the glide (a/2 + b/2, B_0).

# Key Properties

1. p4: point group Z_4, generated by quarter-turns; no reflections or glides
2. p4mm: point group D_4; all mirror directions realized by reflections
3. p4gm: point group D_4; horizontal/vertical reflections replaced by glide reflections
4. p4mm and p4gm are non-isomorphic despite having the same point group D_4 (Theorem 26.3)
5. In p4mm, every order-4 rotation is the product of two reflections in the group
6. In p4gm, some order-4 rotations cannot be written as a product of two reflections in the group
7. p4gm has 8 cosets: H times each element of the point group, with translation offsets (a/2 + b/2) for the B-type matrices

# Construction / Recognition

## p4gm Construction (Armstrong, pp. 167-168)
1. Choose fixed point of order-4 rotation as origin: (0, A_{pi/2}) in G
2. B_0 cannot be realized by reflection; realized by glide (lambda*a + mu*b, B_0)
3. Squaring gives (2*lambda*a, I), so 2*lambda is an integer; must be odd
4. This forces the glide (a/2 + b/2, B_0) to be in G
5. Eight cosets fill out G

# Context & Application

The square lattice groups include the symmetry of square tilings and many architectural patterns. The distinction between p4mm and p4gm (Theorem 26.3) is a beautiful example of how isometry-type preservation (Theorem 25.5) distinguishes wallpaper groups with the same point group.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 167): p4 is generated by two independent translations and a quarter-turn about a lattice point.

**Example 2** (pp. 167-168): In p4gm, elements of the coset H(a/2 + b/2, B_{pi/2}) include reflections in 45-degree mirrors midway between lattice points and glides along lines of gradient one.

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Assuming p4mm and p4gm differ only in which directions have mirrors
  **Correction**: The structural difference is deeper: in p4gm, certain order-4 rotations cannot be factored as products of two reflections in the group

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Thinking "gm" in p4gm means glides replace all mirrors
  **Clarification**: p4gm has both glides and mirrors, but the mirrors are at 45 degrees to the lattice vectors, while horizontal/vertical directions have glides
