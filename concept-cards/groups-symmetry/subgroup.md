# Quick Definition

A subgroup of a group G is a subset of G that itself forms a group under the same operation as G. When H is a subgroup of G, we write H < G.

# Core Definition

"A subgroup of a group G is a subset of G which itself forms a group under the multiplication of G" (Armstrong, Ch. 5, p. 27). To check whether a subset H is a subgroup, one must verify three things: the product of any two elements of H is in H (closure), the identity of G is in H, and the inverse of each element of H is in H. Associativity need not be checked because it is inherited from G.

# Prerequisites

- **Group** — A subgroup is a group contained within another group

# Key Properties

1. H is a subset of G
2. H is closed under the group operation: if x, y are in H, then xy is in H
3. The identity element of G belongs to H
4. If x is in H, then x^{-1} is in H
5. Associativity is inherited from G (no need to check)
6. Every group G has at least two subgroups: {e} (trivial) and G itself
7. When H < G, we say H is a subgroup of G

# Construction / Recognition

## To Verify H < G:
1. Check that H is non-empty
2. Check closure: for all x, y in H, xy is in H
3. Check that e (identity of G) is in H
4. Check that for all x in H, x^{-1} is in H
5. Do NOT need to check associativity

# Context & Application

Armstrong introduces subgroups informally in Chapter 3 (noting Z < R) and formally in Chapter 5. Subgroups are the primary structural tool for understanding groups. Finding all subgroups of a group reveals its internal organization.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 28): Z < Q, Q < R, R < C (chain of subgroups under addition).

**Example 2** (p. 28): The even integers 2Z form a subgroup of Z. More generally, nZ < Z for any positive integer n.

**Example 3** (p. 28): In D_6, the six elements e, r^2, r^4, s, r^2s, r^4s form a copy of D_3 sitting inside D_6.

**Example 4** (p. 28): In D_6, the elements e, r, r^2, r^3, r^4, r^5 form a subgroup (the rotations preserving orientation).

**Example 5** (p. 28): The collection e, r, s, rs does NOT form a subgroup of D_6 because rr = r^2 is missing from the set.

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Forgetting to check closure
  **Correction**: The product of two elements of H must always land back in H

- **Error**: Checking associativity separately for the subgroup
  **Correction**: Associativity is inherited from the ambient group and need not be re-verified

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Thinking any subset of a group is a subgroup
  **Clarification**: A subset must satisfy closure, contain the identity, and contain all inverses to be a subgroup

- **Confusion**: Thinking the identity of a subgroup might differ from the identity of G
  **Clarification**: The identity element of H must be the same as the identity element of G
