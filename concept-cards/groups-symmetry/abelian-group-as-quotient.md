# Quick Definition

A finitely generated abelian group with generators $x_1, \ldots, x_{k+s}$ and relations $m_1 x_1 = 0, \ldots, m_k x_k = 0$ is isomorphic to $A/N$, where $A$ is the free abelian group on the generators and $N$ is the subgroup generated by the relation vectors.

# Core Definition

The standard form of Theorem 21.1 is an abelian group determined by $k + s$ generators $x_1, \ldots, x_{k+s}$ satisfying the relations $m_1 x_1 = 0, \ldots, m_k x_k = 0$. It is isomorphic to the quotient group $A/N$ where $A$ is the free abelian group whose elements are linear combinations $n_1 x_1 + \cdots + n_{k+s} x_{k+s}$ with integer coefficients, and $N$ is the subgroup of $A$ generated by $m_1 x_1, \ldots, m_k x_k$ (Armstrong, p. 131).

# Prerequisites

- **Free abelian group** -- $A$ is a free abelian group
- **Relation between generators** -- $N$ is generated by the relation vectors

# Key Properties

1. $A$ is a free abelian group of rank $k + s$
2. $N$ is a subgroup of $A$ generated by the relation vectors
3. $G \cong A/N$
4. The coefficient matrix encodes the generators of $N$ as a subgroup of $A$
5. Row operations change the generators of $N$; column operations change the generators of $A$

# Context & Application

This perspective bridges the proof of Theorem 21.1 (Chapter 21) with the computational procedure of Chapter 22. It reframes the recognition problem as: given $N$ as a subgroup of $A$ (via the coefficient matrix), find the Smith normal form to read off $A/N$.
