# Quick Definition

A relation between generators $x_1, \ldots, x_r$ of an abelian group is an expression $x_1^{n_1} x_2^{n_2} \cdots x_r^{n_r} = e$. Relations determine the group structure: in additive notation, $n_1 x_1 + n_2 x_2 + \cdots + n_r x_r = 0$.

# Core Definition

An expression of the form $e = x_1^{n_1} x_2^{n_2} \cdots x_r^{n_r}$ is called a **relation** between the generators $x_1, \ldots, x_r$ (Armstrong, p. 127).

In additive notation (Chapter 22), the group $G$ determined by generators $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ and relations $\sum_j a_{ij} x_j = 0$ is isomorphic to $A/N$ where $A$ is the free abelian group on the generators and $N$ is the subgroup generated by the relation vectors (pp. 131--132).

# Key Properties

1. The trivial relation ($n_1 = \cdots = n_r = 0$) always holds
2. If the only relation is trivial, $G \cong \mathbb{Z}^r$ (free abelian)
3. Non-trivial relations constrain the group and introduce torsion
4. Changing generators changes the form of relations but not the group
5. In additive notation, relations are written as $\sum a_{ij} x_j = 0$ and encoded in a coefficient matrix

# Context & Application

Relations, together with generators, present a finitely generated abelian group. The proof of Theorem 21.1 works by simplifying relations through generator changes. Chapter 22 systematises this via matrix row and column operations.
