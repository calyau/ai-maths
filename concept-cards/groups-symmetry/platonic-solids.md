# Quick Definition

There are exactly five convex regular solids: the tetrahedron, cube, octahedron, dodecahedron, and icosahedron.

# Core Definition

"There are five convex regular solids, the *tetrahedron* (four triangular faces), *cube* (six square faces), *octahedron* (eight triangular faces), *dodecahedron* (twelve pentagonal faces), and *icosahedron* (twenty triangular faces)" (Armstrong, p. 44).

# Prerequisites

This is a foundational geometric concept within this chapter.

# Key Properties

1. Exactly five convex regular solids exist
2. Tetrahedron: 4 triangular faces, rotational symmetry group $\cong A_4$
3. Cube: 6 square faces, rotational symmetry group $\cong S_4$
4. Octahedron: 8 triangular faces, rotational symmetry group $\cong S_4$
5. Dodecahedron: 12 pentagonal faces, rotational symmetry group $\cong A_5$
6. Icosahedron: 20 triangular faces, rotational symmetry group $\cong A_5$
7. Cube/octahedron are dual; dodecahedron/icosahedron are dual
8. The tetrahedron is self-dual

# Construction / Recognition

## To Recognize:
1. Check that the solid is convex
2. Check that all faces are congruent regular polygons
3. Check that the same number of faces meet at each vertex

# Context & Application

The Platonic solids are the geometric objects whose symmetry groups Armstrong studies in detail. Their rotational symmetry groups turn out to be isomorphic to familiar permutation groups ($A_4$, $S_4$, $A_5$), providing the most elegant examples of the interplay between geometry and algebra.

# Examples

**Example** (p. 44-48): Armstrong systematically analyzes each solid's rotational symmetries, counting them by identifying axes of symmetry and rotations about each axis.

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Including non-convex regular polyhedra (Kepler-Poinsot solids).
  **Correction**: Armstrong's classification refers only to convex regular solids.

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Thinking the cube and octahedron have different symmetry groups because they look different.
  **Clarification**: They are dual solids and have identical symmetry groups, both isomorphic to $S_4$.
