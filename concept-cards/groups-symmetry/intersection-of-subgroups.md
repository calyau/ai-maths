# Quick Definition

The intersection of two subgroups of a group is itself a subgroup. This follows from the subgroup test: if x and y are in both H and K, then xy^{-1} is in both H and K (since each is a subgroup), so xy^{-1} is in H intersect K.

# Core Definition

"**Theorem.** The intersection of two subgroups of a group is itself a subgroup" (Armstrong, Theorem 5.2, Ch. 5, p. 32). The proof uses Theorem 5.1: the identity lies in both H and K (so H intersect K is non-empty), and if x, y are in H intersect K, then xy^{-1} is in both H and K (since both are subgroups), hence xy^{-1} is in H intersect K.

# Key Properties

1. H intersect K is non-empty (contains at least the identity e)
2. H intersect K is closed under xy^{-1}
3. Therefore H intersect K is a subgroup (by Theorem 5.1)
4. Extends to arbitrary intersections: the intersection of any family of subgroups is a subgroup

# Construction / Recognition

## Proof:
1. e is in both H and K, so H intersect K is non-empty
2. If x, y are in H intersect K, then x, y are in H, so xy^{-1} is in H
3. Similarly, x, y are in K, so xy^{-1} is in K
4. Therefore xy^{-1} is in H intersect K
5. By Theorem 5.1, H intersect K is a subgroup

# Context & Application

This result is fundamental to the lattice theory of subgroups. It guarantees that the intersection of any family of subgroups is a subgroup, which is used to define the subgroup generated by a set as the intersection of all subgroups containing that set.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 28): In Z_6, the subgroups {0, 2, 4} and {0, 3} have intersection {0}, which is a (trivial) subgroup.

**Example 2**: In D_6, the subgroups {e, r^2, r^4, s, r^2s, r^4s} and {e, r, r^2, r^3, r^4, r^5} have intersection {e, r^2, r^4}, which is a subgroup.

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Assuming the union of two subgroups is a subgroup
  **Correction**: The union of two subgroups is generally NOT a subgroup (unless one contains the other)

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Thinking intersection of subgroups might be empty
  **Clarification**: The identity element belongs to every subgroup, so the intersection is always non-empty
