# Quick Definition

The Galois group of $x^4 - 2$ over $\mathbb{Q}$ is $D_4$ (the dihedral group of order 8). This is the main worked example illustrating the Fundamental Theorem of Galois Theory.

# Core Definition

**Example 23.25.** The splitting field of $f(x) = x^4 - 2$ is $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt[4]{2}, i)$ with $[\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt[4]{2}, i):\mathbb{Q}] = 8$. The Galois group $G$ is generated by $\sigma$ (where $\sigma(\sqrt[4]{2}) = i\sqrt[4]{2}$ and $\sigma(i) = i$) and $\tau$ (complex conjugation: $\tau(i) = -i$). The relations $\tau^2 = \text{id}$, $\sigma^4 = \text{id}$, and $\tau\sigma\tau = \sigma^{-1}$ show $G \cong D_4$ (Judson, p. 315).

# Key Properties

1. Splitting field: $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt[4]{2}, i)$ with degree 8 over $\mathbb{Q}$
2. Roots: $\pm\sqrt[4]{2}$ and $\pm i\sqrt[4]{2}$
3. Basis: $\{1, \sqrt[4]{2}, (\sqrt[4]{2})^2, (\sqrt[4]{2})^3, i, i\sqrt[4]{2}, i(\sqrt[4]{2})^2, i(\sqrt[4]{2})^3\}$
4. $G = \{\text{id}, \sigma, \sigma^2, \sigma^3, \tau, \sigma\tau, \sigma^2\tau, \sigma^3\tau\} \cong D_4$
5. The lattice of 10 subgroups of $D_4$ corresponds to 10 intermediate fields

# Context & Application

This is the detailed worked example that illustrates the full power of the Galois correspondence. The subgroup lattice of $D_4$ matches the lattice of intermediate fields, demonstrating all four parts of the Fundamental Theorem.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 315): The lattice of subfields includes $\mathbb{Q}$, $\mathbb{Q}(i)$, $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})$, $\mathbb{Q}(i\sqrt{2})$, $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt[4]{2})$, $\mathbb{Q}(i\sqrt[4]{2})$, $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt[4]{2}, i)$, among others.
