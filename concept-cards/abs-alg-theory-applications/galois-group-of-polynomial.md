# Quick Definition

The Galois group of a polynomial $f(x) \in F[x]$ is the Galois group $G(E/F)$ where $E$ is the splitting field of $f(x)$ over $F$. It is always a subgroup of $S_n$ where $n = \deg f(x)$.

# Core Definition

If $f(x)$ is a polynomial in $F[x]$ and $E$ is the splitting field of $f(x)$ over $F$, then we define the **Galois group of $f(x)$** to be $G(E/F)$ (Judson, p. 308).

If $f(x)$ has no repeated roots, then $|G(E/F)| = [E:F]$ (Theorem 23.7).

# Prerequisites

- **Galois group** — This is the Galois group of the splitting field
- **Splitting field** — The extension is the splitting field of the polynomial

# Key Properties

1. $G(E/F) \leq S_n$ where $n = \deg f(x)$, since automorphisms permute roots
2. $|G(E/F)|$ divides $n!$
3. $f(x)$ is solvable by radicals if and only if $G(E/F)$ is a solvable group
4. For irreducible $f(x)$: $\deg f(x)$ divides $|G(E/F)|$
5. The Galois group of an irreducible quadratic is $\mathbb{Z}_2$
6. The Galois group of an irreducible cubic is $S_3$ or $\mathbb{Z}_3$

# Construction / Recognition

## To Compute:
1. Find the splitting field $E$ of $f(x)$ over $F$
2. Determine $[E:F]$ — this equals $|G(E/F)|$ when $f$ has no repeated roots
3. Identify all automorphisms of $E$ fixing $F$
4. Each automorphism permutes the roots; determine the group structure

# Context & Application

The Galois group of a polynomial is the key to understanding the polynomial's solvability. It determines whether the roots can be expressed using radicals, and its subgroup structure reveals the intermediate fields.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 310): The Galois group of $x^4 + x^3 + x^2 + x + 1$ over $\mathbb{Q}$ is $\mathbb{Z}_4$, generated by $\sigma(\omega) = \omega^2$ where $\omega = e^{2\pi i/5}$.

**Example 2** (p. 315): The Galois group of $x^4 - 2$ over $\mathbb{Q}$ is $D_4$.

**Example 3** (p. 320): The Galois group of $x^5 - 6x^3 - 27x - 3$ over $\mathbb{Q}$ is $S_5$.
