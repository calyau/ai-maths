# Quick Definition

A reflection of the plane is an orientation-reversing isometry that fixes every point of a line (the line of reflection). It is a bilateral symmetry about that line.

# Core Definition

A reflection is an orientation-reversing isometry that is a bilateral symmetry about a line l: it fixes every point of l and maps each point to its mirror image across l (Artin, Theorem 6.3.4(b)(i), p. 169). Algebraically, it has the form t_v * rho_theta * r where the glide vector is zero.

# Prerequisites

- **Isometry** — Reflections are isometries
- **Reflection (linear)** — The linear reflection r about the e_1-axis

# Key Properties

1. Orientation-reversing (determinant of linear part is -1)
2. Fixes a line pointwise (the line of reflection)
3. Has order 2: applying twice gives the identity
4. Composition of two reflections about non-parallel lines is a rotation
5. Composition of two reflections about parallel lines is a translation

# Construction / Recognition

## To Construct:
1. Choose a line l
2. Map each point to its mirror image across l

## To Recognize:
1. An orientation-reversing isometry with a line of fixed points

# Context & Application

Reflections, together with rotations, generate the dihedral groups. The composition of reflections about non-parallel lines yields rotations, providing a geometric way to understand group products. In the isometry group M, every orientation-reversing isometry is either a reflection or a glide reflection.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 169): The standard reflection r about the e_1-axis: r(x_1, x_2) = (x_1, -x_2).

**Example 2** (p. 170): The composition of reflections about two lines intersecting at angle alpha is rotation through 2*alpha about the intersection point.

# Relationships

## Builds Upon
- **Reflection (linear)** — Linear reflection about a line through the origin

## Enables
- **Dihedral group** — Generated by a rotation and a reflection

## Contrasts With
- **Rotation (plane)** — Preserves orientation; reflection reverses it
- **Glide reflection** — Reflection followed by a non-zero parallel translation

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Confusing reflection about a line through the origin with reflection about an arbitrary line
  **Correction**: Reflection about a line not through the origin includes a translation component

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Thinking the composition of two reflections is a reflection
  **Clarification**: The composition of two reflections is orientation-preserving (a rotation or translation)
