# Quick Definition

A group defined by generators S = {x_1, ..., x_n} and relations R = {r_1, ..., r_k} is the quotient G = F/N, where F is the free group on S and N is the normal subgroup of F generated by R. The notation is <x_1, ..., x_n | r_1, ..., r_k>.

# Core Definition

A relation R among elements x_1, ..., x_n of a group G is a word r in the free group on {x_1, ..., x_n} that evaluates to 1 in G (Artin, Definition 7.10.1, p. 224). The group generated by S with relations r_1 = 1, ..., r_k = 1 is the quotient G = F/N, where N is the normal subgroup of F generated by R (Definition 7.10.4, p. 225). If the canonical homomorphism psi: G -> G is bijective, R is called a complete set of relations.

# Prerequisites

- **Free group** — The quotient is of the free group

# Key Properties

1. G = F/N where F is free on S and N is generated by R
2. Notation: <x_1, ..., x_n | r_1, ..., r_k>
3. The canonical map psi: <S | R> -> G is surjective iff S generates G
4. psi is injective iff every relation in G is a consequence of R
5. Computation in G reduces to the word problem in F/N

# Construction / Recognition

## To Define:
1. Choose generators S for the desired group
2. List relations R that the generators satisfy
3. The group is F/N = <S | R>
4. Verify completeness (e.g., via Todd-Coxeter)

# Context & Application

Generators and relations provide a compact description of groups. For example, D_n = <x, y | x^n, y^2, xyxy>. However, determining the structure of a group from its presentation can be difficult (the word problem).

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 224): D_n = <x, y | x^n, y^2, xyxy>.

**Example 2** (p. 225): The tetrahedral group T = <x, y, z | x^3, y^3, z^2, xyz>.

**Example 3** (p. 226): The group <x, y | x^3, y^3, yxyxy> collapses to C_3 (Example 7.11.8).

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Assuming any set of relations defines a nontrivial group
  **Correction**: Too many relations may collapse the group (e.g., Example 7.11.8)
