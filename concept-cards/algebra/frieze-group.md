# Quick Definition

A frieze group is the symmetry group of a frieze pattern -- a figure extending infinitely in one direction with periodic symmetry. Its translation group is infinite cyclic (L = Za), and its classification is left as an exercise.

# Core Definition

The discrete groups G such that the translation group L is infinite cyclic (L = Za for a nonzero vector a) are the symmetry groups of frieze patterns (Artin, p. 184). These groups correspond to patterns that repeat along a strip, such as those in Figures 6.1.3 and 6.1.4.

# Prerequisites

- **Discrete group of isometries** — Frieze groups are a special case
- **Lattice (geometric)** — The 1D case: L = Za

# Key Properties

1. Translation group L = Za (infinite cyclic)
2. Pattern extends infinitely in one direction
3. Classification is an exercise (there are 7 frieze group types)
4. May include rotations by pi, reflections, or glide reflections
