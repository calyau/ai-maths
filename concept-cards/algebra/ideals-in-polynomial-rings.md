# Quick Definition

Every ideal in the polynomial ring F[x] over a field F is a principal ideal, generated by the unique monic polynomial of lowest degree that it contains.

# Core Definition

Every ideal in the ring F[x] of polynomials in one variable over a field F is a principal ideal. A nonzero ideal I in F[x] is generated by the unique monic polynomial of lowest degree that it contains (Artin, Proposition 11.3.22, p. 349).

# Key Properties

1. F[x] is a principal ideal domain
2. The generator is the unique monic polynomial of minimal degree in the ideal
3. The proof mimics the proof that every ideal of Z is principal

# Context & Application

This result makes F[x] a principal ideal domain, which implies it is also a unique factorization domain. It is the polynomial analogue of the fact that every ideal of Z is of the form nZ.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 349): The kernel of the substitution map Q[x] -> C sending x to the cube root of 2 is the principal ideal (x^3 - 2).
