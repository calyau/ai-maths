# Quick Definition

An nth root of unity is a complex number zeta such that zeta^n = 1. The primitive nth roots of unity are those of exact order n, i.e., zeta^k != 1 for 0 < k < n.

# Core Definition

The nth roots of unity are the roots of x^n - 1 in C. The primitive nth root of unity zeta_n = e^{2*pi*i/n} generates all others as powers: the nth roots are 1, zeta_n, zeta_n^2, ..., zeta_n^{n-1}. The primitive roots are those zeta_n^k where gcd(k, n) = 1. For p prime, the irreducible polynomial for zeta_p over Q has degree p-1 (Artin, Section 16.10).

# Prerequisites

- **Field extension** -- Roots of unity live in extension fields

# Key Properties

1. The n-th roots of unity form a cyclic group of order n under multiplication
2. For prime p, zeta_p has degree p-1 over Q
3. The Galois group of Q(zeta_p)/Q is cyclic, isomorphic to (Z/pZ)*
4. Roots of unity are needed as building blocks for Kummer extensions

# Context & Application

Roots of unity are central to Galois theory: they provide the cyclotomic fields, which have abelian Galois groups, and they are needed for the theory of Kummer extensions and solvability by radicals.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 505): zeta_17 = e^{2*pi*i/17} generates Q(zeta_17), a degree-16 extension of Q.

**Example 2** (p. 505): The cube roots of unity 1, omega, omega^2 where omega = e^{2*pi*i/3}.
