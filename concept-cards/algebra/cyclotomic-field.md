# Quick Definition

A cyclotomic field is the field Q(zeta_n) generated over Q by an nth root of unity zeta_n = e^{2*pi*i/n}. For p prime, its Galois group over Q is cyclic of order p-1, isomorphic to F_p*.

# Core Definition

The subfield of C generated over Q by zeta_p = e^{2*pi*i/p} (p prime) is a cyclotomic field. The irreducible polynomial for zeta over Q is x^{p-1} + ... + x + 1 (Artin, p. 505, equation 16.10.1). Its Galois group is cyclic of order p-1, isomorphic to F_p* (Proposition 16.10.2). Every automorphism sends zeta to a power zeta^k.

- **Field extension** -- Cyclotomic fields are specific extensions of Q

# Key Properties

1. The Galois group G(Q(zeta_p)/Q) is cyclic of order p-1, isomorphic to F_p* (16.10.2(a))
2. The automorphism sigma_k sends zeta to zeta^k
3. For any subfield F' of C, G(F'(zeta)/F') is cyclic (16.10.2(b))
4. The unique quadratic subfield is Q(sqrt(p)) if p = 1 mod 4, Q(sqrt(-p)) if p = 3 mod 4 (16.10.12)
5. Kronecker-Weber: every abelian Galois extension of Q is contained in a cyclotomic field (16.10.13)

# Context & Application

Cyclotomic fields are the most important examples of Galois extensions with abelian (specifically cyclic) Galois groups. They are used to prove constructibility of regular polygons and to analyze Kummer extensions.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 505-508): For p = 17, zeta = e^{2*pi*i/17}. The Galois group is cyclic of order 16 generated by sigma: zeta -> zeta^3. The chain of subgroups gives fields Q in Q(sqrt(17)) in L_2 in L_3 in Q(zeta), each of degree 2. This proves the regular 17-gon is constructible (Corollary 16.10.5).

**Example 2** (p. 508): The discriminant of (x - alpha_1)(x - alpha_2) = x^2 + x - 4 for p = 17 is 17, so L_1 = Q(sqrt(17)).
