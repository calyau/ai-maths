# Quick Definition

The free group F on a set S is the group of reduced words in the symbols S and their formal inverses, with concatenation (followed by cancellation) as the group operation. Its elements satisfy no relations other than the group axioms.

# Core Definition

Let S = {a, b, c, ...} be a set, and let S' = {a, a^{-1}, b, b^{-1}, ...}. A word in S' is reduced if no cancellation xx^{-1} or x^{-1}x can be made. There is a unique reduced form for every word (Proposition 7.9.2). The set of equivalence classes of words (two words are equivalent if they have the same reduced form) forms a group under juxtaposition (Artin, Proposition 7.9.4, p. 224). This is the free group on S.

# Prerequisites

This concept is foundational within the context of generators and relations.

# Key Properties

1. Elements are reduced words in S and S^{-1}
2. Multiplication is concatenation followed by cancellation
3. Every reduced word is unique (Proposition 7.9.2)
4. Products of equivalent words are equivalent (Proposition 7.9.3)
5. Free on one element = infinite cyclic group
6. Free on two or more elements is non-abelian and very large
7. Mapping property: any set map S -> G extends uniquely to a homomorphism F -> G (Proposition 7.10.12)

# Construction / Recognition

## To Construct:
1. Start with a set S of symbols
2. Form all finite strings using S and formal inverses S^{-1}
3. Identify strings that reduce to the same reduced word
4. Multiply by concatenation and reduction

# Context & Application

Free groups are the universal groups on a set of generators -- they have no relations other than the group axioms. They are the starting point for defining groups by generators and relations: any group with generators S satisfying relations R is a quotient F/N where F is the free group on S and N is the normal subgroup generated by R.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 223): Free group on one element {a} is the infinite cyclic group Z.

**Example 2** (p. 223): In the free group on {a, b}, the reduced word abb^{-1}c^{-1}cb = ab^2 after cancellation. Multiplication: (abc^{-1})(cb) = abc^{-1}cb = abb.
