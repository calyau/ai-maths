# Quick Definition

The Galois group G(K/F) of a field extension K/F is the group of all F-automorphisms of K -- the automorphisms of K that fix every element of F. It encodes the symmetries of the extension.

# Core Definition

Let K and K' be extension fields of F. An F-isomorphism sigma: K -> K' is an isomorphism whose restriction to F is the identity map. An F-automorphism of K is an F-isomorphism from K to itself. The F-automorphisms of a finite extension K form a group called the Galois group of K over F, denoted G(K/F) (Artin, p. 492). A finite extension K/F is a Galois extension if |G(K/F)| = [K:F].

# Prerequisites

- **Field extension** -- The Galois group is defined for field extensions
- **Splitting field** -- Galois extensions are splitting fields
- **Group** -- The Galois group is a group under composition

# Key Properties

1. The order of G(K/F) always divides [K:F] (Lemma 16.6.2(a))
2. K/F is Galois iff |G(K/F)| = [K:F] iff K is a splitting field over F iff the fixed field K^G = F (Theorem 16.6.4)
3. G operates on the roots of any polynomial with coefficients in F that splits in K (16.6.6)
4. If f is irreducible over F, G acts transitively on its roots in K (16.6.6(c))
5. An F-automorphism is determined by its action on generators of K (16.4.2(b))

# Construction / Recognition

## To Construct:
1. Find a primitive element gamma for K/F
2. Determine the irreducible polynomial f for gamma over F
3. Find all roots of f that lie in K, say gamma_1, ..., gamma_r
4. Each root gives a unique F-automorphism sigma_i with sigma_i(gamma) = gamma_i
5. G(K/F) = {sigma_1, ..., sigma_r} (Lemma 16.6.3)

## To Recognize:
1. Verify K/F is a finite extension
2. Count F-automorphisms; compare with [K:F]

# Context & Application

The Galois group is the central object of Galois theory. Through the Fundamental Theorem, it controls the structure of intermediate fields. Through its action on roots, it determines the solvability of polynomial equations by radicals.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 492): G(C/R) is cyclic of order 2, generated by complex conjugation.

**Example 2** (p. 492): For K = Q(sqrt(2)), G(K/Q) has order 2, with the nontrivial element sending a + b*sqrt(2) to a - b*sqrt(2).

**Example 3** (p. 499): For the splitting field of an irreducible cubic f over F: G = A_3 (cyclic of order 3) if the discriminant is a square in F; G = S_3 if not (Theorem 16.8.5).

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Computing automorphisms by permuting roots without checking they extend to field automorphisms
  **Correction**: Not every permutation of roots comes from an automorphism. Only those consistent with the field structure count.

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Thinking the Galois group always equals the full symmetric group on the roots
  **Clarification**: G embeds as a subgroup of S_n; it equals S_n only in special cases (e.g., "generic" polynomials).
