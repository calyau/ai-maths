# Quick Definition

The Main Lemma states that for any nonzero ideal A in the ring of integers of an imaginary quadratic field, the product A*A-bar is a principal ideal (n) generated by a positive integer n. This is the key tool for proving unique factorization of ideals.

# Core Definition

Let R be the ring of integers in an imaginary quadratic number field. The product of a nonzero ideal A and its complex conjugate A-bar is a principal ideal generated by a positive ordinary integer n: A*A-bar = (n) = nR (Artin, Lemma 13.4.8, p. 403).

# Prerequisites

- **Ideal multiplication** -- The product A*A-bar
- **Ring of integers** -- Working in the ring of algebraic integers
- **Norm (quadratic field)** -- The integer n is the norm of A

# Key Properties

1. A*A-bar is always principal
2. The proof uses the fact that certain combinations of lattice basis elements are ordinary integers
3. Would be FALSE for rings smaller than R (e.g., missing half-integer elements)
4. Enables the cancellation law for ideals (Corollary 13.4.9)

# Context & Application

This lemma is the engine driving all the main results of Chapter 13: cancellation of ideals, unique factorization of ideals, and the finiteness of the class group.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 402): In Z[sqrt(-5)], A = (2, 1+delta): A*A-bar = (4, 2+2delta, 2-2delta, 6) = (2).
