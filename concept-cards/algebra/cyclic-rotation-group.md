# Quick Definition

The cyclic rotation group C_n is the group of order n generated by the rotation through angle 2pi/n about the origin. It consists of all rotational symmetries of a regular n-gon.

# Core Definition

C_n is the cyclic group of order n generated by the rotation rho_theta, where theta = 2pi/n (Artin, Theorem 6.4.1(a), p. 174). Every finite subgroup of O_2 consisting entirely of rotations is cyclic.

# Prerequisites

- **Rotation matrix** — Generated by rho_{2pi/n}

# Key Properties

1. Order n, generated by rotation through 2pi/n
2. Abelian (cyclic)
3. Every finite subgroup of O_2 with only rotations is of this form
4. Subgroup of index 2 in D_n

# Construction / Recognition

## To Construct:
1. Choose integer n >= 1
2. C_n = {1, rho, rho^2, ..., rho^{n-1}} where rho = rho_{2pi/n}

## To Recognize:
1. A finite group of rotations about a single point

# Context & Application

Cyclic rotation groups are the simplest finite symmetry groups. They appear as subgroups of dihedral groups and in the classification of finite subgroups of O_2. A figure with only rotational symmetry (no reflections) has C_n as its symmetry group.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 174): C_1 = {1} is the trivial group.

**Example 2** (p. 174): C_2 = {1, rho_pi}, where rho_pi is rotation by 180 degrees.

# Relationships

## Builds Upon
- **Rotation matrix** — Elements are powers of rho_{2pi/n}

## Enables
- **Dihedral group** — C_n is the rotation subgroup of D_n

## Contrasts With
- **Dihedral group** — D_n adds reflections to C_n

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Confusing C_n as a subgroup of O_2 with the abstract cyclic group Z/nZ
  **Clarification**: They are isomorphic, but C_n here specifically refers to a group of rotation matrices
