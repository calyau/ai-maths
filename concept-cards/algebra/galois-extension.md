# Quick Definition

A finite extension K/F is a Galois extension if the order of its Galois group equals the degree of the extension: |G(K/F)| = [K:F]. Equivalently, K is a splitting field over F, or the fixed field of G(K/F) is exactly F.

# Core Definition

A finite extension K/F is a Galois extension if the order of its Galois group G(K/F) is equal to the degree of the extension: |G(K/F)| = [K:F] (Artin, p. 492). The following are equivalent (Theorem 16.6.4): (a) K/F is Galois; (b) the fixed field K^G = F; (c) K is a splitting field over F.

# Prerequisites

- **Galois group** -- Must know what G(K/F) is
- **Splitting field** -- Equivalent characterization

# Key Properties

1. Every finite extension is contained in a Galois extension (16.6.5(a))
2. If K/F is Galois and L is intermediate, then K/L is also Galois (16.6.5(b))
3. G operates faithfully on roots of any polynomial that defines K as a splitting field (16.6.6(b))
4. If g is irreducible over F, G acts transitively on its roots in K (16.6.6(c))
5. L/F is Galois iff the corresponding subgroup H of G is normal; if so, G(L/F) = G/H (Theorem 16.7.5)

# Context & Application

Galois extensions are the extensions where the full machinery of Galois theory applies. The equivalence with splitting fields provides a concrete construction, while the equivalence with the fixed field condition provides theoretical power.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 492): C/R is Galois of degree 2, with G generated by conjugation.

**Example 2** (p. 499): The splitting field of x^3 - 3x + 1 over Q has [K:Q] = 3 and G = A_3 (Galois).

**Example 3** (p. 499): Q(cube_root(2))/Q is NOT Galois: it has degree 3 but G has order 1 (no nontrivial automorphisms, since the other cube roots are complex).
