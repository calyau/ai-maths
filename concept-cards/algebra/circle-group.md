# Quick Definition
The circle group is the group of complex numbers of absolute value 1, equivalently U_1 or SO_2. It has dimension 1 and is the simplest compact connected Lie group.
# Core Definition
The unit circle {x_0^2 + x_1^2 = 1} has several group incarnations: the additive group of angles (via cos theta, sin theta), the multiplicative group U_1 = {p in C* | p-bar p = 1} (9.1.6), and the rotation group SO_2 via the embedding (cos theta, sin theta) -> [[cos theta, -sin theta],[sin theta, cos theta]] (9.1.7) (Artin, pp. 265-266).
# Prerequisites
- **Classical groups** — The circle group is U_1 = SO_2
# Key Properties
1. U_1 = SO_2 = S^1 (topologically)
2. Dimension 1
3. Abelian
4. Compact and connected
5. Lie algebra is R (one-dimensional)
# Examples
**Example 1** (p. 265): e^{i theta} for theta in [0, 2pi) parametrizes U_1.
