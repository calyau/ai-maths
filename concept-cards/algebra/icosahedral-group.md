# Quick Definition

The icosahedral group I is the group of 60 rotational symmetries of a regular dodecahedron (or icosahedron). It is isomorphic to the alternating group A_5 and is a simple group.

# Core Definition

The icosahedral group I consists of the 60 rotational symmetries of a dodecahedron or icosahedron (Artin, Theorem 6.12.1, p. 194). Its class equation is 60 = 1 + 20 + 12 + 12 + 15 (Artin, (7.4.1), p. 209), and it is isomorphic to A_5 (Theorem 7.4.4). It is a simple group (Theorem 7.4.3).

# Prerequisites

- **Finite subgroups of rotation group** — I is one of the five types

# Key Properties

1. Order 60
2. Class equation: 60 = 1 + 20 + 12 + 12 + 15
3. Simple group (no proper normal subgroups)
4. Isomorphic to A_5
5. Pole data: r_i = 2, 3, 5; n_i = 30, 20, 12

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 209): Conjugacy classes: identity (1), rotations about vertices (20), face rotations by 2pi/5 (12), face rotations by 4pi/5 (12), edge rotations by pi (15).

**Example 2** (p. 211): I is isomorphic to A_5 via its action on 5 cubes inscribed in a dodecahedron.
