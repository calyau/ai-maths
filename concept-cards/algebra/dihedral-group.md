# Quick Definition

The dihedral group D_n is the group of symmetries of a regular n-sided polygon, of order 2n, generated by a rotation x of angle 2pi/n and a reflection y satisfying x^n = 1, y^2 = 1, yx = x^{-1}y.

# Core Definition

The dihedral group D_n has order 2n. It is generated by two elements x and y satisfying the relations x^n = 1, y^2 = 1, yx = x^{-1}y (Artin, Proposition 6.4.3, p. 174). The elements are 1, x, x^2, ..., x^{n-1}; y, xy, x^2y, ..., x^{n-1}y. When n >= 3, D_n is the group of symmetries of a regular n-sided polygon.

# Prerequisites

- **Rotation matrix** — The rotation generator x = rho_{2pi/n}
- **Reflection (linear)** — The reflection generator y = r
- **Cyclic rotation group** — C_n is the rotation subgroup of D_n

# Key Properties

1. Order 2n
2. Generated by rotation x (order n) and reflection y (order 2)
3. Key relation: yx = x^{-1}y (equivalently xyxy = 1)
4. Contains C_n as a subgroup of index 2
5. D_3 is isomorphic to S_3 (Corollary 6.4.5)
6. For n >= 3, D_n is the symmetry group of a regular n-gon

# Construction / Recognition

## To Construct:
1. Take a rotation x of angle 2pi/n and any reflection y
2. The group generated by x and y is D_n

## To Recognize:
1. A group of order 2n with an element of order n and an element of order 2
2. Satisfying the relation yx = x^{-1}y

# Context & Application

Dihedral groups are the most important finite symmetry groups. They arise as symmetry groups of regular polygons, as finite subgroups of O_2 (Theorem 6.4.1), and as subgroups of the rotation group SO_3 (Theorem 6.12.1). They are also important in the presentation of groups by generators and relations (Section 7.10).

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 174): D_3 = S_3, with x = (123) and y = (23).

**Example 2** (p. 175): D_1 = {1, r} is a cyclic group of order 2. D_2 = {1, rho_pi, r, rho_pi * r} is isomorphic to the Klein four group.

**Example 3** (p. 174): The relation yx = x^{-1}y is equivalent to xyxy = 1, and can also be written yx = x^{n-1}y.

# Relationships

## Builds Upon
- **Cyclic rotation group** — C_n is the rotation subgroup

## Enables
- **Crystallographic restriction** — Limits n to 1, 2, 3, 4, 6
- **Generators and relations** — D_n = <x, y | x^n, y^2, xyxy>

## Contrasts With
- **Cyclic rotation group** — C_n has only rotations; D_n also has reflections

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Confusing D_n with C_{2n}
  **Correction**: D_n has order 2n but is non-abelian for n >= 3; C_{2n} is cyclic and abelian

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Different conventions for D_n -- some authors use D_{2n} for the same group
  **Clarification**: Artin uses D_n for the dihedral group of order 2n (symmetries of an n-gon)
