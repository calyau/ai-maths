# Quick Definition

The coordinate ring of a variety V defined by polynomials f_1, ..., f_r is the quotient ring R = C[x_1, ..., x_n]/(f_1, ..., f_r). Its maximal ideals correspond bijectively to points of V.

# Core Definition

Let I be the ideal of C[x_1, ..., x_n] generated by polynomials f_1, ..., f_r, and let R = C[x_1, ..., x_n]/I be the quotient ring. Let V be the variety of common zeros of f_1, ..., f_r. The maximal ideals of R are in bijective correspondence with the points of V (Artin, Theorem 11.9.1, p. 368).

# Prerequisites

- **Algebraic variety** -- V is a variety
- **Quotient ring** -- R is a quotient of a polynomial ring

# Key Properties

1. R = C[x]/I is the ring of "polynomial functions" on V
2. Algebraic properties of R reflect geometric properties of V
3. R = 0 iff V is empty iff 1 is in I

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 352): C[x, y]/(y^2 - x^3 + 1) is the coordinate ring of an elliptic curve.

**Example 2** (p. 354): C[t]/(t^2-1) has four ideals, corresponding to the variety {1, -1}.
