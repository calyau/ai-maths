# Quick Definition

A rotation of the plane is an orientation-preserving isometry that fixes exactly one point (the center of rotation) and rotates the plane through some angle about that point. It has the form t_a * rho_theta for suitable a and theta.

# Core Definition

An isometry t_a * rho_theta with theta != 0 is a rotation through the angle theta about a point p in the plane (Artin, Lemma 6.3.5, p. 170). The point p is the unique fixed point, found by solving (I - rho_theta)p = a. Every orientation-preserving isometry that is not a translation is a rotation about some point (Theorem 6.3.4(a)(ii)).

# Prerequisites

- **Isometry** — Rotations are orientation-preserving isometries
- **Rotation matrix** — The linear part rho_theta

# Key Properties

1. Fixes exactly one point p (the center of rotation)
2. Orientation-preserving (determinant of linear part is 1)
3. Has the form t_a * rho_theta with theta != 0
4. The center p satisfies (I - rho_theta)p = a
5. Composition of two rotations about different points is a rotation (or translation)
6. Conjugate to rotation about origin: t_p * rho_theta * t_{-p}

# Construction / Recognition

## To Construct:
1. Choose a center point p and angle theta
2. The isometry is t_p * rho_theta * t_{-p}

## To Find the Center:
1. Write the isometry as t_a * rho_theta
2. Solve (I - rho_theta)p = a (solvable when theta != 0)

# Context & Application

Rotations (along with translations) are the orientation-preserving isometries of the plane. The composition of reflections about non-parallel lines is a rotation about their intersection point. Rotation groups of regular polygons are the cyclic groups C_n.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 170): The fixed point of t_a * rho_theta is found geometrically: the line through the origin perpendicular to a is bisected by the sector of angle theta, and the vector a is inserted into the sector.

**Example 2** (p. 170): The composition of rotations about two distinct points is a rotation about a third point (unless the total angle is 0, giving a translation).

- **Cyclic rotation group** — Generated by a rotation of angle 2pi/n

## Contrasts With
- **Translation** — Has no fixed point
- **Reflection (isometry)** — Reverses orientation

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Confusing rotation about the origin with rotation about another point
  **Correction**: Rotation about p is conjugation: t_p * rho_theta * t_{-p}, not just rho_theta

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Thinking the composition of two rotations about different points could be a reflection
  **Clarification**: Composition of rotations is always orientation-preserving, hence a rotation or translation
