# Quick Definition

A maximal ideal M of a ring R is a proper ideal not contained in any larger proper ideal. Equivalently, R/M is a field.

# Core Definition

A maximal ideal M of a ring R is an ideal that is not equal to R, and that is not contained in any ideal other than M and R (Artin, p. 363). An ideal I is maximal if and only if R/I is a field (Proposition 11.8.2).

# Prerequisites

- **Ideal** -- A maximal ideal is a special type of ideal
- **Field** -- The quotient by a maximal ideal is a field

# Key Properties

1. R/M is a field if and only if M is maximal
2. The zero ideal of R is maximal if and only if R is a field
3. Every maximal ideal is a prime ideal
4. Every nonzero ring has at least one maximal ideal (Theorem 11.9.2)

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 363): The maximal ideals of Z are (p) for prime p.

**Example 2** (p. 363): The maximal ideals of F[x] are generated by monic irreducible polynomials.

**Example 3** (p. 363): The maximal ideals of C[x] are (x - a) for a in C (Corollary 11.8.5).
