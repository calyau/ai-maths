# Quick Definition

A field extension K/F is a pair of fields where F is a subfield of K. The notation K/F indicates that K is an extension field of F, or equivalently, F is a subfield of K.

# Core Definition

Much of the theory of fields has to do with a pair F contained in K of fields, one contained in the other. Given such a pair, K is called a field extension of F, or an extension field. The notation K/F indicates that K is a field extension of F (Artin, p. 453).

# Prerequisites

- **Field** -- Both F and K must be fields
- **Subfield** -- F is a subfield of K

# Key Properties

1. K can always be regarded as an F-vector space
2. The dimension [K:F] of K as an F-vector space is the degree of the extension
3. A finite extension is generated by finitely many algebraic elements (15.3.6(c))
4. Three important classes: number fields, finite fields, function fields (p. 453)

# Construction / Recognition

## To Construct:
1. Start with a field F and an irreducible polynomial f(x) in F[x]
2. Form K = F[x]/(f) to adjoin a root of f
3. More generally, adjoin any elements to F and take the smallest field containing them

## To Recognize:
1. Verify both F and K are fields
2. Verify F is a subfield of K

# Context & Application

Field extensions are the central objects of study in Chapters 15-16. They connect to polynomial roots (adjoining roots creates extensions), Galois theory (automorphisms of extensions), and classical problems like ruler-and-compass constructions.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 453): C is a field extension of R with [C:R] = 2 and basis (1, i).

**Example 2** (p. 453): Q(sqrt(2))/Q is a quadratic extension. Every number field is an extension of Q.

**Example 3** (p. 453): Finite fields F_{p^r} are extensions of the prime field F_p.

# Relationships

## Builds Upon
- **Field** -- Both components are fields

## Enables
- **Algebraic element** -- Elements algebraic over F
- **Degree of field extension** -- The dimension [K:F]
- **Galois group** -- Automorphisms of the extension
- **Splitting field** -- Extension where a polynomial splits completely

## Related
- **Simple extension** -- K = F(alpha) for one element alpha

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: The notation K/F suggesting a quotient
  **Clarification**: K/F denotes an extension (K contains F), not a quotient. This is standard notation in field theory.
