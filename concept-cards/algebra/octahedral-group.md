# Quick Definition

The octahedral group O is the group of 24 rotational symmetries of a cube or regular octahedron. Its pole data is r_i = 2, 3, 4; n_i = 12, 8, 6; N = 24.

# Core Definition

The octahedral group O consists of the 24 rotational symmetries of a cube or octahedron (Artin, Theorem 6.12.1, p. 194). The cube and octahedron are dual polyhedra and thus have the same symmetry group. Poles: 12 at edge centers (r=2), 8 at vertices (r=3), 6 at face centers (r=4).

# Prerequisites

- **Finite subgroups of rotation group** — O is one of the five types

# Key Properties

1. Order 24
2. Isomorphic to S_4
3. The cube and octahedron have the same rotation group (duality)
4. Contains the tetrahedral group T as a subgroup

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 193): The octahedral group acts on the 6 faces of a cube; pairs of opposite faces form an orbit of size 3 with stabilizer of order 8.
