# Quick Definition
The coordinate ring of an affine algebraic set V ⊆ A^n is the quotient ring k[V] = k[A^n]/I(V), where I(V) is the ideal of all polynomials vanishing on V.

# Core Definition
If V ⊆ A^n is an affine algebraic set, the quotient ring k[A^n]/I(V) is called the **coordinate ring of V**, denoted k[V] (Definition, p. 675). The elements of k[V] are the restrictions of polynomial functions from A^n to V. Two polynomials f and g define the same function on V if and only if f - g ∈ I(V). The coordinate ring k[V] is a finitely generated k-algebra.

# Prerequisites
- **affine-algebraic-set** — V must be an algebraic set
- **quotient-ring** — k[V] is defined as a quotient

# Key Properties
1. k[V] is a finitely generated k-algebra generated by the coordinate functions x̄_1,...,x̄_n
2. Elements of k[V] represent polynomial functions V → k
3. Two polynomials give the same element of k[V] iff they agree on V
4. Morphisms V → W correspond bijectively to k-algebra homomorphisms k[W] → k[V] (Theorem 6)
5. V is a variety iff k[V] is an integral domain (Corollary 18)

# Construction / Recognition
## To Construct:
1. Determine I(V), the ideal of polynomials vanishing on V
2. Form the quotient k[x_1,...,x_n]/I(V)

# Context & Application
The coordinate ring is the fundamental algebraic invariant of an algebraic set. The equivalence between morphisms of algebraic sets and k-algebra homomorphisms of coordinate rings (Theorem 6, p. 676) is a cornerstone of algebraic geometry that allows geometric problems to be solved algebraically.

# Examples
**Example** (p. 675): If V = Z(xy-1) is the hyperbola y = 1/x in R^2, then R[V] = R[x,y]/(xy-1) ≅ R[x,1/x].

# Common Errors
- **Error**: Confusing k[V] with k[A^n]. **Correction**: k[V] is a quotient of k[A^n]; relations in I(V) hold in k[V].

# Common Confusions
- **Confusion**: Thinking an isomorphism of coordinate rings implies the algebraic sets are the same subset of A^n. **Clarification**: Isomorphic coordinate rings mean the algebraic sets are isomorphic, but they may sit in different ambient spaces.
