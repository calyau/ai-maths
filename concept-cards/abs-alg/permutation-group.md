# Quick Definition
A permutation group is a subgroup of a symmetric group S_n. By Cayley's Theorem, every group is isomorphic to a permutation group.

# Core Definition
Subgroups of symmetric groups are called permutation groups. For any subgroup G of S_n, the orbits of G refer to its orbits on {1, 2, ..., n} (Dummit & Foote, p. 117). By Cayley's Theorem (Corollary 4, p. 120), every group is isomorphic to a permutation group.

# Prerequisites
- **Symmetric group** — A permutation group is a subgroup of S_n
- **Subgroup** — Must be a subgroup

# Key Properties
1. Every group is isomorphic to some permutation group (Cayley's Theorem)
2. The orbits of a permutation group partition {1,...,n}
3. Historically, groups were first studied as permutation groups
