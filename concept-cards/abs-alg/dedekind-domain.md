# Quick Definition
A Dedekind domain is a Noetherian, integrally closed, integral domain of Krull dimension 1. Every PID is a Dedekind domain, and rings of integers in number fields are Dedekind domains.

# Core Definition
A **Dedekind Domain** is a Noetherian, integrally closed, integral domain of Krull dimension 1 (Definition, p. 764). Equivalently (Theorem 15): (1) Noetherian + integrally closed + all nonzero primes maximal, (2) Noetherian + all localizations at nonzero primes are DVRs, (3) every nonzero fractional ideal is invertible, (4) every nonzero fractional ideal is projective, (5) every nonzero proper ideal factors uniquely into prime ideals.

# Prerequisites
- **noetherian-ring** — Must be Noetherian
- **integral-closure** — Must be integrally closed
- **krull-dimension** — Must have Krull dimension 1

# Key Properties
1. Every PID is a Dedekind domain (Proposition 14)
2. Rings of integers O_K are Dedekind domains (Proposition 14)
3. Unique factorization of ideals into prime ideals (Theorem 15(5), Corollary 16)
4. Every ideal can be generated by at most 2 elements (Corollary 19)
5. R is a PID iff R is a UFD (Corollary 20)
6. Chinese Remainder Theorem holds (Proposition 18)

# Examples
**Example** (p. 764): Z, k[x], Z[√-5] (via ring of integers) are Dedekind domains. Z[√-5] is not a PID.
