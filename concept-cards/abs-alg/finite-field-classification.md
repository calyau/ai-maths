# Quick Definition
For every prime p and positive integer n, there exists a unique (up to isomorphism) field with $p^n$ elements, denoted $\mathbb{F}_{p^n}$ or $GF(p^n)$. It is the splitting field of $x^{p^n} - x$ over $\mathbb{F}_p$.

# Core Definition
(Theorem, p. 586) For every prime p and integer $n \geq 1$, there is exactly one field (up to isomorphism) of order $p^n$. This field $\mathbb{F}_{p^n}$ is the splitting field of $x^{p^n} - x$ over $\mathbb{F}_p$, consisting of all roots of this polynomial. The extension $\mathbb{F}_{p^n}/\mathbb{F}_p$ is Galois with $\text{Gal}(\mathbb{F}_{p^n}/\mathbb{F}_p) \cong \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$, generated by the Frobenius automorphism $\sigma_p: x \mapsto x^p$. Furthermore, $\mathbb{F}_{p^m} \subseteq \mathbb{F}_{p^n}$ iff $m \mid n$ (Dummit & Foote, pp. 586-591).

# Key Properties
1. There exists a unique field of order $p^n$ for each prime p and $n \geq 1$
2. $\mathbb{F}_{p^n}^{\times}$ is cyclic of order $p^n - 1$
3. $\text{Gal}(\mathbb{F}_{p^n}/\mathbb{F}_p) = \langle\sigma_p\rangle \cong \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$
4. $\mathbb{F}_{p^m} \subseteq \mathbb{F}_{p^n}$ iff $m \mid n$
5. Every finite field is perfect (every extension is separable)
