# Quick Definition
The commutator of elements x and y in a group G is [x, y] = x^{-1}y^{-1}xy. Two elements commute if and only if their commutator is the identity. The commutator of subgroups A and B is the group generated by all [a, b] with a in A, b in B.

# Core Definition
Let G be a group, let x, y in G and let A, B be nonempty subsets of G. (1) [x, y] = x^{-1}y^{-1}xy is the commutator of x and y. (2) [A, B] = <[a, b] | a in A, b in B> is the commutator of subsets A and B. (3) G' = <[x, y] | x, y in G> is the commutator subgroup of G (Dummit & Foote, p. 175). By Proposition 7(1), xy = yx[x,y], so [x,y] = 1 iff x and y commute.

# Prerequisites
- **Group** — Commutators are defined in any group

# Key Properties
1. [x, y] = 1 iff x and y commute
2. xy = yx[x, y] (Proposition 7(1))
3. [y, x] = [x, y]^{-1}
4. H is normal in G iff [H, G] <= H (Proposition 7(2))
5. sigma([x, y]) = [sigma(x), sigma(y)] for any automorphism sigma

# Examples
**Example 1** (p. 177): In D_{2n}, [r, s] = r^{-2}, so D_{2n}' = <r^2>.
