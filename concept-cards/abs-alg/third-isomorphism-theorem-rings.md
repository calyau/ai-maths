# Quick Definition
If $I \subseteq J$ are ideals of R, then $J/I$ is an ideal of $R/I$ and $(R/I)/(J/I) \cong R/J$.

# Core Definition
(Theorem 8(2)) Let I and J be ideals of R with $I \subseteq J$. Then $J/I$ is an ideal of $R/I$ and $(R/I)/(J/I) \cong R/J$ (Dummit & Foote, p. 248).

# Prerequisites
- **Ideal** — Both I and J must be ideals with $I \subseteq J$
- **Quotient ring** — The theorem involves iterated quotients

# Key Properties
1. "Quotient of a quotient is a quotient"
2. Allows simplification of iterated quotient rings
