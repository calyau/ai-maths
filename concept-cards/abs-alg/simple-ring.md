# Quick Definition
A simple ring is a ring whose only two-sided ideals are 0 and the whole ring.

# Core Definition
A ring whose only two-sided ideals are 0 and the whole ring is called a *simple ring*. If R is commutative with identity, $R$ is simple iff R is a field (Proposition 9(2)). For noncommutative rings: $M_n(F)$ is simple for any field F and $n \geq 1$ (Dummit & Foote, p. 254).

# Prerequisites
- **Ring with identity** — Simple rings are typically required to have identity
- **Ideal** — Simplicity is defined via the ideal structure

# Key Properties
1. Commutative simple ring = field (Proposition 9(2))
2. $M_n(F)$ is simple for any field F (p. 254)
3. $M_n(F)$ for $n \geq 2$ is simple but not a division ring (has zero divisors)
4. Simple rings are studied in Chapter 18
