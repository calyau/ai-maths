# Quick Definition
For a group G acting on a set A, the stabilizer of an element a in A is the subgroup of G consisting of all elements that fix a: G_a = {g in G | g . a = a}. The stabilizer is always a subgroup of G.

# Core Definition
For each a in A, the stabilizer of a in G is the set of elements of G that fix the element a: G_a = {g in G | g . a = a}, which is a subgroup of G (Dummit & Foote, p. 112). The kernel of the action is contained in every stabilizer since it is the intersection of all stabilizers. If b = g . a for some g in G, then the stabilizers of a and b are conjugate: G_b = gG_a g^{-1} (Exercise 1, p. 120).

# Prerequisites
- **Group action** — Stabilizers are defined relative to an action
- **Subgroup** — The stabilizer is always a subgroup of G

# Key Properties
1. G_a is a subgroup of G for each a in A
2. The kernel of the action is the intersection of all stabilizers
3. Conjugate elements have conjugate stabilizers: if b = g . a, then G_b = gG_a g^{-1}
4. For transitive actions, all stabilizers are conjugate to each other
5. |orbit of a| = |G : G_a| (the Orbit-Stabilizer Theorem)

# Construction / Recognition
## To Compute the Stabilizer G_a:
1. Fix an element a in A
2. Find all g in G such that g . a = a
3. Verify this set forms a subgroup (guaranteed by theory)

# Context & Application
Stabilizers are central to the Orbit-Stabilizer Theorem, which relates orbit sizes to subgroup indices. In the conjugation action, the stabilizer of an element is its centralizer; the stabilizer of a subset is its normalizer. Stabilizers appear throughout the Sylow theorems.

# Examples
**Example 1** (p. 112): D_8 acts on the four vertices of a square. The stabilizer of vertex 1 is <s>, the subgroup of order 2 generated by the reflection through vertex 1 and the center.

**Example 2** (p. 114): S_n acts on {1,...,n}; the stabilizer of i is isomorphic to S_{n-1}.

# Common Errors
- **Error**: Assuming all stabilizers in a given action are equal
  **Correction**: Stabilizers of different points are generally different (though conjugate in transitive actions)

# Common Confusions
- **Confusion**: Confusing stabilizer with centralizer
  **Clarification**: The centralizer is the stabilizer specifically for the conjugation action; for other actions, the stabilizer may mean something different
