# Quick Definition
The commutator subgroup G' = [G, G] is the subgroup generated by all commutators [x, y] = x^{-1}y^{-1}xy. It is characteristic in G, and G/G' is the largest abelian quotient of G.

# Core Definition
G' = <[x, y] | x, y in G>, the subgroup of G generated by commutators of elements from G, is called the commutator subgroup of G (Dummit & Foote, p. 175). By Proposition 7: G' is characteristic in G; G/G' is abelian; G' is the smallest normal subgroup with abelian quotient; H is normal in G with G/H abelian iff G' <= H. Any homomorphism from G to an abelian group factors through G'.

# Prerequisites
- **Commutator** — G' is generated by commutators
- **Normal subgroup** — G' is normal (in fact characteristic) in G
- **Characteristic subgroup** — G' is invariant under all automorphisms

# Key Properties
1. G' is characteristic in G (Proposition 7(3))
2. G/G' is abelian (Proposition 7(3))
3. G/G' is the LARGEST abelian quotient of G (Proposition 7(4))
4. G' <= H iff H is normal and G/H is abelian (Proposition 7(4))
5. G is abelian iff G' = 1
6. G' need not consist only of single commutators (it's the group they generate)

# Examples
**Example 1** (p. 177): D_8' = Z(D_8) = <r^2>. Q_8' = Z(Q_8) = <-1>.

**Example 2** (p. 177): D_{2n}' = <r^2>. If n is odd, D_{2n}' = <r>; if n is even, |D_{2n}'| = n/2.

**Example 3** (p. 178): S_n' = A_n for n >= 5.
