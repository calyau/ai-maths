# Quick Definition
The trivial representation sends every group element to the identity on a 1-dimensional space: φ(g) = 1 for all g. Its character is the constant function 1.

# Core Definition
The **trivial representation** of G is the degree 1 representation φ: G → GL_1(F) defined by φ(g) = 1 for all g ∈ G (Example 1, p. 841). It is not faithful when |G| > 1.
