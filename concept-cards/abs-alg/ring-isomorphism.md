# Quick Definition
A ring isomorphism is a bijective ring homomorphism. Two rings are isomorphic ($R \cong S$) if there exists a ring isomorphism between them.

# Core Definition
A bijective ring homomorphism is called an *isomorphism*. If A and B are rings, $A \cong B$ will always mean an isomorphism of rings unless otherwise stated (Dummit & Foote, p. 240).

# Prerequisites
- **Ring homomorphism** — Must be a ring homomorphism that is bijective

# Key Properties
1. Isomorphic rings have identical algebraic structure
2. The inverse of a ring isomorphism is also a ring isomorphism
3. $R/\ker\varphi \cong \varphi(R)$ for any ring homomorphism $\varphi$ (First Isomorphism Theorem)
