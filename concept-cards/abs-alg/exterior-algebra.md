# Quick Definition
The exterior algebra $\bigwedge(M) = \mathcal{T}(M)/\mathcal{A}(M)$ is the quotient of the tensor algebra by the ideal generated by $m \otimes m$ for all $m \in M$. Products $m_1 \wedge m_2$ are anticommutative.

# Core Definition
The exterior algebra of M is $\bigwedge(M) = \mathcal{T}(M)/\mathcal{A}(M)$ where $\mathcal{A}(M)$ is the ideal generated by all $m \otimes m$. The image of $m_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes m_k$ is written $m_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge m_k$ (wedge product). The relation $m \wedge m = 0$ implies $m_1 \wedge m_2 = -m_2 \wedge m_1$. The kth exterior power $\bigwedge^k(V)$ has dimension $\binom{n}{k}$ for n-dimensional V. In particular, $\bigwedge^n(V)$ is 1-dimensional and $\bigwedge^k(V) = 0$ for $k > n$ (Dummit & Foote, pp. 449-455).

# Key Properties
1. $m \wedge m = 0$ and $m_1 \wedge m_2 = -m_2 \wedge m_1$
2. $\dim_F \bigwedge^k(V) = \binom{n}{k}$ for n-dimensional V
3. $\bigwedge^n(V) \cong F$ (1-dimensional) and $\bigwedge^k(V) = 0$ for $k > n$
4. Universal property for alternating multilinear maps
5. Basis: $v_{i_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge v_{i_k}$ with $i_1 < i_2 < \cdots < i_k$
6. The determinant is the induced map on $\bigwedge^n(V)$
