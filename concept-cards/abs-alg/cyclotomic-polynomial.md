# Quick Definition
The nth cyclotomic polynomial $\Phi_n(x)$ is the minimal polynomial over $\mathbb{Q}$ of a primitive nth root of unity. It has degree $\phi(n)$ (Euler's totient) and is irreducible over $\mathbb{Q}$.

# Core Definition
A primitive nth root of unity is a complex number $\zeta$ with $\zeta^n = 1$ and $\zeta^k \neq 1$ for $0 < k < n$. The nth cyclotomic polynomial is $\Phi_n(x) = \prod (x - \zeta)$ where the product runs over all primitive nth roots of unity. Key facts: $x^n - 1 = \prod_{d|n} \Phi_d(x)$, $\Phi_n(x) \in \mathbb{Z}[x]$, and $\Phi_n(x)$ is irreducible over $\mathbb{Q}$ (Dummit & Foote, pp. 554-558).

# Key Properties
1. $\deg \Phi_n(x) = \phi(n)$
2. $\Phi_n(x) \in \mathbb{Z}[x]$ (integer coefficients)
3. $\Phi_n(x)$ is irreducible over $\mathbb{Q}$
4. $x^n - 1 = \prod_{d \mid n} \Phi_d(x)$
5. $[\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n):\mathbb{Q}] = \phi(n)$
6. $\text{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n)/\mathbb{Q}) \cong (\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^{\times}$
