# Quick Definition
The symmetric algebra $\mathcal{S}(M) = \mathcal{T}(M)/\mathcal{C}(M)$ is the quotient of the tensor algebra by the ideal generated by $m_1 \otimes m_2 - m_2 \otimes m_1$. It is the universal commutative R-algebra generated by M.

# Core Definition
The symmetric algebra of M is $\mathcal{S}(M) = \mathcal{T}(M)/\mathcal{C}(M)$ where $\mathcal{C}(M)$ is the ideal generated by all $m_1 \otimes m_2 - m_2 \otimes m_1$. It is commutative and graded. For a free module of rank n, $\mathcal{S}(M) \cong R[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$, the polynomial ring (Dummit & Foote, pp. 447-449).

# Prerequisites
- **tensor-algebra** — The symmetric algebra is a quotient of the tensor algebra

# Key Properties
1. $\mathcal{S}(M)$ is commutative
2. Universal property: module maps from M to commutative algebras factor through $\mathcal{S}(M)$
3. For $V = F^n$: $\mathcal{S}(V) \cong F[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$
4. $\mathcal{S}^k(M)$ is the kth symmetric power of M
5. $\dim_F \mathcal{S}^k(V) = \binom{n+k-1}{k}$ for n-dimensional V
