# Quick Definition
The Axiom of Choice states that the Cartesian product of any nonempty collection of nonempty sets is nonempty — i.e., a choice function always exists.

# Core Definition
**The Axiom of Choice**: The Cartesian product of any nonempty collection of nonempty sets is nonempty. Equivalently, if I is any nonempty set and A_i is nonempty for all i ∈ I, then there exists a choice function from I to ∪A_i (p. 908). By Theorem 2, this is equivalent to Zorn's Lemma and the Well Ordering Principle.

# Key Properties
1. Equivalent to Zorn's Lemma (Theorem 2)
2. Equivalent to the Well Ordering Principle (Theorem 2)
3. Independent of the Zermelo-Fraenkel axioms
4. Used throughout algebra (existence of bases, maximal ideals, etc.)
