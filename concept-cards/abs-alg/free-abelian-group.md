# Quick Definition
The free abelian group of rank r is Z^r = Z x Z x ... x Z (r copies). It is the group with presentation <s_1, ..., s_r | [s_i, s_j] = 1 for all i, j>. It has the universal property: any set map from the generators to an abelian group extends uniquely to a homomorphism.

# Core Definition
For each r >= 0, Z^r = Z x Z x ... x Z is the direct product of r copies of Z, called the free abelian group of rank r (Dummit & Foote, p. 158). Z^0 = 1. The free abelian group on a set S is the group with presentation (S, {[s,t] | s, t in S}), i.e., the free group on S made abelian. By Exercise 11 (p. 224), if A is a free abelian group on n generators, then A is isomorphic to Z^n.

# Prerequisites
- **Free group** — Free abelian is the abelianization of the free group
- **Direct product** — Z^r is a direct product

# Key Properties
1. Z^r is finitely generated by r elements
2. Universal property: any set map to an abelian group extends to a unique homomorphism
3. Z^r is free abelian of rank r iff it has a basis of r elements
4. The rank r appears in the Fundamental Theorem as the Betti number
