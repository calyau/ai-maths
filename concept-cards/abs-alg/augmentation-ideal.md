# Quick Definition
The augmentation ideal of a group ring RG is the kernel of the augmentation map $\sum a_ig_i \mapsto \sum a_i$, consisting of elements whose coefficients sum to zero.

# Core Definition
The map from the group ring RG to R defined by $\sum_{i=1}^n a_ig_i \mapsto \sum_{i=1}^n a_i$ is a surjective ring homomorphism called the *augmentation map*. Its kernel, the *augmentation ideal*, consists of elements of RG whose coefficients sum to 0. The quotient is isomorphic to R (Dummit & Foote, p. 247).

# Prerequisites
- **Group ring** — The augmentation ideal is defined for group rings
- **Ideal** — It is an ideal of RG

# Key Properties
1. Generated by $\{g - 1 \mid g \in G\}$ (Exercise 2, p. 254)
2. If $G = \langle \sigma \rangle$ is cyclic, the augmentation ideal is generated by $\sigma - 1$ (Exercise 2)
3. $RG/(\text{augmentation ideal}) \cong R$
4. If F is a field, the augmentation ideal of FG is a maximal ideal (p. 256)

# Examples
**Example**: In $\mathbb{Z}D_8$, the element $r - s$ (where $r, s$ are generators of $D_8$) lies in the augmentation ideal.
