# Quick Definition
The two vertices incident with an edge are its endvertices (or ends). An edge joins its ends.

# Core Definition
The two vertices incident with an edge are its endvertices or ends, and an edge joins its ends (Diestel, p. 3).

# Prerequisites
- **Edge** — endvertices are the two vertices that define an edge

# Key Properties
1. Every edge has exactly two endvertices
2. An edge e = xy has endvertices x and y

# Construction / Recognition
For edge e = xy, the endvertices are x and y.

# Context & Application
The concept of endvertices is used throughout graph theory when discussing the structure of edges, paths, and cycles.
