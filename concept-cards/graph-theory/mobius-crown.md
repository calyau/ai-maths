# Quick Definition
A Mobius crown is the 1-skeleton of a triangulation of the Mobius strip. It is one of the four types of unavoidable minors in large 4-connected graphs.

# Core Definition
A **Mobius crown** is the 1-skeleton of a triangulation of the Mobius strip (Fig. 9.4.1). It is one of the four types of unavoidable minors in large 4-connected graphs (Theorem 9.4.4). (Diestel, p. 269)

# Prerequisites
- **Graph** — Basic graph concepts

# Key Properties
1. Non-orientable surface graph (Mobius strip)
2. Triangulated: every face is a triangle
3. Non-planar (unlike the crown)
4. One of four unavoidable types in 4-connected graphs
