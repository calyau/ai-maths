# Quick Definition
The axiom of choice states that for any collection of non-empty sets, there exists a function choosing one element from each set. It is assumed throughout Diestel's text and is equivalent to Zorn's lemma and the well-ordering theorem.

# Core Definition
The axiom of choice is assumed throughout the text. One of its many equivalent statements is the well-ordering theorem: every set can be well-ordered. It is also equivalent to Zorn's lemma (Diestel, p. 358).

# Prerequisites
This is a foundational axiom.

# Key Properties
1. Equivalent to Zorn's lemma
2. Equivalent to the well-ordering theorem
3. Needed for transfinite recursive definitions involving choices
4. Assumed throughout Diestel's Graph Theory

# Context & Application
The axiom of choice is essential in infinite combinatorics. Recursive definitions in Chapter 8 that involve choosing vertices from a graph rely on it.
