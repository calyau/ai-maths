# Quick Definition
The order of a bramble is the minimum number of vertices needed to cover (hit) every element of the bramble. The bramble number (max order) equals tw(G) + 1.

# Core Definition
The *order* of a bramble B is the minimum number of vertices covering B. The *bramble number* of G is the largest order of any bramble in G. By Theorem 12.3.9, the bramble number equals tw(G) + 1 (Diestel, p. 332).
