# Quick Definition
The cut space C*(G) is the subspace of the edge space generated by all cuts (sets of edges crossing a vertex partition). For a connected graph with n vertices, dim C*(G) = n - 1.

# Core Definition
If {V_1, V_2} is a partition of V, the set E(V_1, V_2) of all the edges of G crossing this partition is called a cut (or cocycle). Together with the empty set, the cuts in G form a subspace C* of E(G). This space is generated by cuts of the form E(v) (Proposition 1.9.3) (Diestel, pp. 24-25).

# Prerequisites
- **Graph**, **edge-space** — the cut space is a subspace of the edge space
- **Cut** — generated by cuts

# Key Properties
1. C* is generated by cuts of the form E(v) (Proposition 1.9.3)
2. C* = C-perp (Theorem 1.9.5): cut space is the orthogonal complement of the cycle space
3. For a connected graph: dim C*(G) = n - 1 (Theorem 1.9.6)
4. dim C(G) + dim C*(G) = m
5. Minimal non-empty elements of C* are bonds

# Context & Application
The cut space is dual to the cycle space. Together they decompose the edge space into orthogonal complements.
