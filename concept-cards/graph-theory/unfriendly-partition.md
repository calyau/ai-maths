# Quick Definition
A bipartition of V(G) is unfriendly if every vertex has at least as many neighbours in the other class. The conjecture that every countable graph has one is open.

# Core Definition
A bipartition of V(G) is *unfriendly* if every vertex has at least as many neighbours in the other class as in its own. The Unfriendly Partition Conjecture: every countable graph admits such a partition. Proved for locally finite graphs by compactness (Diestel, p. 213).
