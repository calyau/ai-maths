# Quick Definition
A boundary circle of a subset X of a surface S is a component of the frontier of X that is a circle (homeomorphic to S^1). A boundary circle of a disc bounds that disc.

# Core Definition
"A component of the frontier of X that is a circle in S is a boundary circle of X. A boundary circle of a disc in S is said to bound that disc" (Diestel, p. 361).

# Prerequisites
- **Surface**

# Key Properties
1. Boundary circles are components of frontiers that are circles
2. Used in handle and crosscap attachment operations
3. Surgery involves capping boundary circles with discs

# Relationships
## Related
- **Surgery** -- Capping boundary circles after cutting
- **Handle** -- Attached along boundary circles
