# Quick Definition
The age of a countable graph is the class of all finite graphs that are isomorphic to induced subgraphs of G. Fraïssé's theorem characterizes which ages correspond to homogeneous graphs.

# Core Definition
The age of a countable graph G is the class of all finite graphs isomorphic to induced subgraphs of G. This concept connects graph theory to model theory via Fraïssé's theorem (Diestel, p. 225, notes).
