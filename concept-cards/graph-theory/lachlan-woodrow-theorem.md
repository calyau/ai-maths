# Quick Definition
Theorem 8.3.3 (1980) classifies all countably infinite homogeneous graphs: disjoint unions of equal complete graphs or their complements, R^r or its complement for r >= 3, or the Rado graph R.

# Core Definition
**Theorem 8.3.3** (Lachlan & Woodrow 1980): Every countably infinite homogeneous graph is one of: disjoint unions of same-order complete graphs (or complement), R^r (or complement) for r >= 3, or the Rado graph (Diestel, p. 225).
