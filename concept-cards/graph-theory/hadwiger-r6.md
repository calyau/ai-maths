# Quick Definition
Hadwiger's conjecture for r = 6 was proved by Robertson, Seymour & Thomas (1993). The proof shows any minimal counterexample arises from a planar graph by adding one vertex, so the four colour theorem applies.

# Core Definition
**Theorem 7.3.7** (Robertson, Seymour & Thomas 1993): Hadwiger's conjecture holds for r = 6. The proof shows (without using the four colour theorem) that any minimal-order counterexample arises from a planar graph by adding one vertex—so by the four colour theorem it is not a counterexample after all. This case is "substantially more difficult" than r = 5. (Diestel, p. 175)

1. The most difficult proved case
