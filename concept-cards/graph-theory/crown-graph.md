# Quick Definition
A crown is the 1-skeleton of a triangulation of the cylinder. It is one of the four types of unavoidable minors in large 4-connected graphs.

# Core Definition
A **crown** is the 1-skeleton of a triangulation of the cylinder as shown in Fig. 9.4.1. It is one of the four types of unavoidable minors in large 4-connected graphs (Theorem 9.4.4). (Diestel, p. 269)

# Prerequisites
- **Graph** — Basic graph concepts

# Key Properties
1. Planar graph (can be drawn on a cylinder)
2. Triangulated: every face is a triangle
3. One of four unavoidable types in 4-connected graphs
