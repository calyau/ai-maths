# Quick Definition
The self-minor conjecture (Seymour, 1980s) states that every countably infinite graph is a proper minor of itself, where a proper minor requires at least one non-trivial branch set.

# Core Definition
Call H a *proper minor* of G if G contains an MH with at least one non-trivial branch set. **Self-minor conjecture** (Seymour 1980s): Every countably infinite graph is a proper minor of itself (Diestel, p. 359).

# Key Properties
1. Easily implies the graph minor theorem for finite graphs (Exercise 44)
2. Disproved for uncountable graphs (Oporowski 1990)
3. Open for countable graphs
4. Related to whether countable graphs are WQO by minors
