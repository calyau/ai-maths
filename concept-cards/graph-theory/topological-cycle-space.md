# Quick Definition
The topological cycle space C(G) of a locally finite graph G consists of all sums of thin families of circuits, where a family is thin if no edge lies in infinitely many of its members, and the sum consists of edges appearing an odd number of times.

# Core Definition
Call a family (D_i)_{i in I} of subsets of E(G) *thin* if no edge lies in D_i for infinitely many i. The *sum* of this family is the set of all edges that lie in D_i for an odd number of indices i. The (topological) cycle space C(G) of G is the subspace of its edge space consisting of all sums of thin families of circuits. C(G) is closed under addition and agrees with the finite cycle space when G is finite (Diestel, p. 242).

# Key Properties
1. C(G) contains precisely those edge sets meeting every finite cut evenly (Theorem 8.5.8(i))
2. Every element of C(G) is a disjoint sum of circuits (Theorem 8.5.8(ii))
3. The fundamental circuits of any topological spanning tree generate C(G) (Theorem 8.5.8(iii))
4. C(G) is generated by finite circuits (Corollary 8.5.9)
5. C(G) is closed under infinite thin sums (Corollary 8.5.9)

# Context & Application
The topological cycle space extends the finite cycle space to locally finite infinite graphs in a way that preserves all the key properties (characterization by cuts, decomposition into circuits, generation by fundamental circuits).

# Examples
**Example 1** (p. 243): The cycle space of the 2-way infinite ladder is generated by all its squares, or equivalently by the infinite circuit of horizontal edges together with all squares but one.
