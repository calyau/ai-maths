# Quick Definition
The cycle space C(G) is the subspace of the edge space spanned by all cycles in G. For a connected graph with n vertices and m edges, dim C(G) = m - n + 1.

# Core Definition
The cycle space C = C(G) is the subspace of E(G) spanned by all the cycles in G — more precisely, by their edge sets. The dimension of C(G) is sometimes called the cyclomatic number of G (Diestel, p. 23).

# Prerequisites
- **Graph**, **cycle**, **edge-space** — the cycle space is a subspace generated by cycles

# Key Properties
1. C(G) is spanned by all cycles in G
2. The induced cycles generate the entire cycle space (Proposition 1.9.1)
3. An edge set F is in C(G) iff F is a disjoint union of cycles iff all vertex degrees in (V, F) are even (Proposition 1.9.2)
4. C = C*-perp (Theorem 1.9.5): cycle space is the orthogonal complement of the cut space
5. For a connected graph: dim C(G) = m - n + 1 (Theorem 1.9.6)

# Context & Application
The cycle space captures the algebraic structure of cycles in a graph. Together with the cut space, it completely decomposes the edge space.
