# Quick Definition
The Freudenthal compactification of a connected locally finite graph G is the topological space |G| obtained by adding ends as limit points. It is compact and Hausdorff (Proposition 8.5.1).

# Core Definition
When G is connected and locally finite, |G| is a compact Hausdorff space (Proposition 8.5.1). Topologists call |G| the *Freudenthal compactification* of G (Diestel, p. 238).

# Prerequisites
- **Topological end space** — |G| is the Freudenthal compactification
- **Locally finite graph** — Compactness requires local finiteness

# Key Properties
1. |G| is compact iff G is locally finite (for connected G)
2. Hausdorff always
3. Metrizable iff G has a normal spanning tree (Theorem 8.5.2)
4. Rays converge to their ends in this topology
