# Quick Definition

The real span $\mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}} = \sum_{\alpha \in R} \mathbb{R} h_\alpha \subset \mathfrak{h}$ satisfies $\mathfrak{h} = \mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}} \oplus i\mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}}$, and the Killing form restricted to $\mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}}$ is positive definite. This makes the root system a finite set in a real Euclidean space.

# Core Definition

**Theorem 7.22** (Kirillov, p. 89):
1. $\mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}} \subset \mathfrak{h}$ is a real form: $\mathfrak{h} = \mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}} \oplus i\mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}}$, and the Killing form restricted to $\mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}}$ is positive definite.
2. $\mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}}^* \subset \mathfrak{h}^*$ (generated by roots) satisfies $\mathfrak{h}^* = \mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}}^* \oplus i\mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}}^*$.

# Prerequisites

- **Cartan subalgebra** — $\mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}}$ is a real form of $\mathfrak{h}$
- **Coroot of Lie algebra** — The $h_\alpha$ span $\mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}}$
- **Killing form on $\mathfrak{h}$** — Must be positive definite on $\mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}}$

# Key Properties

1. $(h_\alpha, h_\beta) = \sum_{\gamma \in R} \langle h_\alpha, \gamma \rangle \langle h_\beta, \gamma \rangle \in \mathbb{Z}$ (all integers!)
2. $(h, h) = \sum_\gamma \langle h, \gamma \rangle^2 \geq 0$ for $h \in \mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}}$, with equality only for $h = 0$
3. $\dim_{\mathbb{R}} \mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}} = r = \operatorname{rank}(\mathfrak{g})$

# Context & Application

This theorem shows the root system lives in a real Euclidean space $\mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}}^*$ with a positive definite inner product. This is the geometric setting required for the classification of root systems and Dynkin diagrams.

# Examples

**Example**: For $\mathfrak{sl}(n, \mathbb{C})$: $\mathfrak{h}_{\mathbb{R}} = \{\text{real diagonal traceless matrices}\}$, and the Killing form restricted to it is $2n \sum h_i^2 > 0$.
