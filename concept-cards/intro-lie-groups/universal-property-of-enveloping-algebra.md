# Quick Definition

The universal enveloping algebra $U\mathfrak{g}$ has the universal property: any Lie algebra homomorphism $\rho: \mathfrak{g} \to A$ (where $A$ is an associative algebra, viewed as a Lie algebra) extends uniquely to an algebra homomorphism $U\mathfrak{g} \to A$.

# Core Definition

**Theorem 4.53** (Kirillov, p. 51): Let $A$ be an associative algebra with unit over $K$ and let $\rho: \mathfrak{g} \to A$ be a linear map such that $\rho(x)\rho(y) - \rho(y)\rho(x) = \rho([x,y])$. Then $\rho$ can be uniquely extended to a morphism of associative algebras $U\mathfrak{g} \to A$.

**Corollary 4.54**: Categories of representations of $\mathfrak{g}$ and of $U\mathfrak{g}$-modules are equivalent.

# Prerequisites

- **Universal enveloping algebra** — The algebra with this property

# Key Properties

1. Justifies the name "universal": $U\mathfrak{g}$ is the "most general" associative algebra generated by $\mathfrak{g}$
2. Any representation $\rho: \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{gl}(V)$ extends to $U\mathfrak{g} \to \mathrm{End}(V)$
3. This converts Lie algebra representation theory into module theory over an associative algebra
