# Quick Definition

A distribution $\mathcal{D}$ on a manifold is completely integrable if and only if the space of vector fields tangent to $\mathcal{D}$ is closed under the commutator of vector fields.

# Core Definition

**Theorem 3.46** (Kirillov, Frobenius): Let $\mathcal{D}$ be a distribution on $M$. Then $\mathcal{D}$ is completely integrable if and only if the space of vector fields tangent to $\mathcal{D}$ is closed under commutator.

# Prerequisites

- **Lie algebra of vector fields** — the commutator of vector fields

# Key Properties

1. A $k$-dimensional distribution is a smooth assignment of $k$-dimensional subspaces $\mathcal{D}_p \subset T_pM$.
2. An integral manifold is a submanifold $X$ with $T_pX = \mathcal{D}_p$ at every point.
3. Completely integrable means local integral manifolds exist through every point.
4. The closure condition on brackets is both necessary and sufficient.

# Construction / Recognition

## To Construct/Create:
1. Define a distribution $\mathcal{D}$.
2. Check that brackets of tangent vector fields remain tangent to $\mathcal{D}$.

## To Identify/Recognize:
1. A distribution whose tangent fields are closed under the Lie bracket.

# Context & Application

Frobenius theorem is the key analytic tool used in the proof of Theorem 3.43 (subalgebra-subgroup correspondence). The subalgebra $\mathfrak{h}$ defines a distribution $\mathcal{D}^{\mathfrak{h}}_p = \mathfrak{h} \cdot p$ on $G$, and the subalgebra condition ensures integrability.

# Examples

**Example** (Lemma 3.47, p. 32): The distribution $\mathcal{D}^{\mathfrak{h}}_p = \mathfrak{h} \cdot p$ on $G$ is generated by right-invariant vector fields corresponding to elements of $\mathfrak{h}$. Since $\mathfrak{h}$ is closed under bracket, so are these fields (Corollary 3.25), hence the distribution is integrable.

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Assuming any distribution is integrable.
  **Correction**: Only those satisfying the Frobenius condition. For $k = 1$ (direction fields), integrability is automatic (existence of integral curves).

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Whether Frobenius applies only in the Lie group context.
  **Clarification**: Frobenius is a general differential geometry result applicable to any manifold.
