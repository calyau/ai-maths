# Quick Definition

The universal enveloping algebra $U\mathfrak{g}$ is the associative algebra generated by elements of $\mathfrak{g}$ subject to the relation $xy - yx = [x,y]$. It converts Lie algebra representations into modules over an associative algebra.

# Core Definition

**Definition 4.50** (Kirillov, p. 51): Let $\mathfrak{g}$ be a Lie algebra over $K$. The universal enveloping algebra $U\mathfrak{g}$ is the associative algebra with unit over $K$ with generators $i(x)$, $x \in \mathfrak{g}$, subject to relations $i(x+y) = i(x) + i(y)$, $i(cx) = ci(x)$, $c \in K$, and

$$i(x)i(y) - i(y)i(x) = i([x,y]).$$

Equivalently, $U\mathfrak{g} = T\mathfrak{g}/(xy - yx - [x,y])$, the quotient of the tensor algebra by the two-sided ideal generated by $xy - yx - [x,y]$ for $x, y \in \mathfrak{g}$.

# Key Properties

1. **Universal property** (Theorem 4.53): Any linear map $\rho: \mathfrak{g} \to A$ (associative algebra) satisfying $\rho(x)\rho(y) - \rho(y)\rho(x) = \rho([x,y])$ extends uniquely to $U\mathfrak{g} \to A$
2. **Corollary 4.54**: Categories of representations of $\mathfrak{g}$ and $U\mathfrak{g}$-modules are equivalent
3. $U\mathfrak{g}$ is infinite-dimensional (unless $\mathfrak{g} = 0$)
4. $\mathfrak{g} \hookrightarrow U\mathfrak{g}$ is injective (Corollary 4.62, from PBW)
5. $U\mathfrak{g}$ has no zero divisors (Corollary 4.64)
6. For commutative $\mathfrak{g}$: $U\mathfrak{g} \cong S\mathfrak{g} \cong K[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ (Example 4.51)

# Construction / Recognition

## To Construct:
1. Start with the tensor algebra $T\mathfrak{g} = \bigoplus_{n \geq 0} \mathfrak{g}^{\otimes n}$
2. Quotient by the ideal generated by $xy - yx - [x,y]$ for $x, y \in \mathfrak{g}$
3. The result is $U\mathfrak{g}$

## Key Warning:
Multiplication in $U\mathfrak{g}$ differs from matrix multiplication even when $\mathfrak{g} \subset \mathfrak{gl}(n)$. For example, $e^2 \neq 0$ in $U\mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{C})$ even though $e^2 = 0$ as a $2 \times 2$ matrix.

# Context & Application

$U\mathfrak{g}$ lets us work with products of Lie algebra elements in a representation-independent way. Central elements of $U\mathfrak{g}$ (like the Casimir operator) act as scalars on irreducibles by Schur's lemma, providing powerful tools for decomposing representations.

# Examples

**Example 4.51** (p. 51): For a commutative Lie algebra, $U\mathfrak{g} = K[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$.

**Example 4.52** (p. 51): $U\mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{C})$ is generated by $e, f, h$ with relations $ef - fe = h$, $he - eh = 2e$, $hf - fh = -2f$.

**Example 4.55** (p. 52): The Casimir element $C = ef + fe + \frac{1}{2}h^2 \in U\mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{C})$ is central.

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Thinking $e^2 = 0$ in $U\mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{C})$ because $e^2 = 0$ as a matrix.
  **Correction**: Multiplication in $U\mathfrak{g}$ is abstract; $e^2 \neq 0$ in $U\mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{C})$ because there exist representations where $\rho(e)^2 \neq 0$.

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Thinking $U\mathfrak{g}$ is finite-dimensional.
  **Clarification**: $U\mathfrak{g}$ is always infinite-dimensional (for $\mathfrak{g} \neq 0$), even though $\mathfrak{g}$ is finite-dimensional.
