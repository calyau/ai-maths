# Quick Definition

In a connected Lie group $G$, any neighborhood $U$ of the identity generates the entire group. This means every element of $G$ can be written as a finite product of elements from $U$.

# Core Definition

**Corollary 2.9** (Kirillov):
1. If $G$ is a connected Lie group and $U$ is a neighborhood of $1$, then $U$ generates $G$.
2. If $f: G_1 \to G_2$ is a morphism with $f_*: T_1G_1 \to T_1G_2$ surjective and $G_2$ connected, then $f$ is surjective.

# Prerequisites

- **Lie group** — the group in question
- **Connected component of identity** — must be connected
- **Lie subgroup** — used in the proof (the subgroup generated by $U$ is open and closed)

# Key Properties

1. The subgroup generated by $U$ is open (since $hU$ is open for any $h$ in the subgroup).
2. Being open in a manifold, it is a submanifold, hence a Lie subgroup.
3. By the closed subgroup theorem, it is closed.
4. Being open, closed, and non-empty in a connected space, it must be all of $G$.

# Context & Application

This result is used repeatedly throughout the text. It is the key reason why the exponential map (which only gives a neighborhood of the identity) determines the entire connected group. Used in Proposition 3.9 to show morphisms of connected groups are determined by their Lie algebra maps.

# Examples

**Example** (Proposition 3.9, p. 22): A morphism $\varphi: G_1 \to G_2$ of connected groups is determined by $\varphi_*$, because $\varphi_*$ determines $\varphi$ near $1$ (via exp), and a neighborhood of $1$ generates $G_1$.

**Example 3.10** (p. 22): Rotations $\exp(tJ_x), \exp(tJ_y), \exp(tJ_z)$ generate all of $\mathrm{SO}(3, \mathbb{R})$.

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Applying this to disconnected groups.
  **Correction**: A neighborhood of $1$ generates only $G^0$ (the identity component), not all of $G$.

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Whether "generates" means the same as in abstract group theory.
  **Clarification**: Yes, exactly: every element of $G$ is a finite product of elements from $U$ and their inverses.
