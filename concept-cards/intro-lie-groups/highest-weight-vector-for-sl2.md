# Quick Definition

A highest weight vector in a representation of $\mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{C})$ is a non-zero vector $v \in V[\lambda]$ where $\lambda$ is the highest weight. Equivalently, $v$ is annihilated by $e$: $ev = 0$.

# Core Definition

(Kirillov, p. 59): Vectors $v \in V[\lambda]$ where $\lambda$ is the highest weight are called highest weight vectors.

**Lemma 5.4**: Let $v \in V[\lambda]$ be a highest weight vector. Then:
1. $ev = 0$
2. Define $v^k = \frac{f^k}{k!}v$, $k \geq 0$. Then:
   - $hv^k = (\lambda - 2k)v^k$
   - $fv^k = (k+1)v^{k+1}$
   - $ev^k = (\lambda - k + 1)v^{k-1}$ for $k > 0$

# Prerequisites

- **Highest weight for sl(2,C)** — The weight of the vector
- **Raising and lowering operators** — $e$ annihilates the highest weight vector; $f$ generates lower weight vectors

# Key Properties

1. $ev = 0$ (there is no weight space $V[\lambda + 2]$)
2. All other weight vectors are obtained by applying powers of $f$
3. The vectors $v^0, v^1, v^2, \ldots$ span an invariant subspace
4. For irreducible $V$: the highest weight vector is unique up to scalar
5. For $V = V_n$: $v^0, v^1, \ldots, v^n$ form a basis

# Construction / Recognition

## To Identify:
1. Diagonalize $h$ to find weight vectors
2. Find the vector(s) in the highest weight space
3. Equivalently: find $v \neq 0$ with $ev = 0$ and $hv = \lambda v$

# Context & Application

The highest weight vector generates the entire irreducible representation by repeated application of $f$. This is the prototype for the highest-weight construction in all semisimple Lie algebras.

# Examples

**Lemma 5.4** (p. 59): Starting from $v = v^0$ with $hv = \lambda v$, $ev = 0$, the vectors $v^k = f^k v / k!$ give a complete basis for the representation (in the irreducible case).
