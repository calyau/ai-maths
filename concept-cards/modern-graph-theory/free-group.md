# Quick Definition

The free group $\langle a, b, c, \ldots \rangle$ on generators $a, b, c, \ldots$ consists of equivalence classes of words under the relation that $xx^{-1}$ and $x^{-1}x$ may be inserted or deleted, with multiplication given by juxtaposition.

# Core Definition

A word $W$ in symbols $a, b, c, \ldots$ is a finite sequence such as $ba^{-1}ccaa^{-1}b^{-1}a$. Two words are equivalent if one is obtained from the other by repeatedly replacing $xx^{-1}$ or $x^{-1}x$ by the empty word, or vice versa. The equivalence classes form a group under juxtaposition: $(ab^{-1}c)(c^{-1}ba) = a^2$. This is the free group generated by $a, b, c, \ldots$, denoted $\langle a, b, c, \ldots \rangle$ (Bollobas, p. 260).

# Key Properties

1. No relations other than $xx^{-1} = x^{-1}x = 1$
2. The identity is the empty word
3. Inverse of $a^{-1}b^{-1}c$ is $c^{-1}ba$
4. The rank $k$ (number of generators) is well-defined
5. Cayley diagram of a free group is a tree

# Construction / Recognition

## To Work in a Free Group
1. Write elements as reduced words (no adjacent $xx^{-1}$ pairs)
2. Multiply by juxtaposition followed by cancellation of $xx^{-1}$ pairs
3. The inverse reverses the order and inverts each symbol

# Context & Application

Free groups are the starting point for group presentations: every group is a quotient of a free group. The Nielsen-Schreier theorem (Theorem 4) states that subgroups of free groups are free, with rank computable from the Schreier diagram.

# Examples

**Example** (p. 260): In $\langle a, b, c \rangle$: $abb^{-1}a^{-1}c^{-1} = c^{-1}$ and $(ab^{-1}c)(c^{-1}ba) = ab^{-1}cc^{-1}ba = a^2$.

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Writing non-reduced words as group elements
  **Correction**: Always reduce by canceling adjacent inverse pairs

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Thinking a free group on two generators is abelian
  **Clarification**: In $\langle a, b \rangle$, $ab \ne ba$; the free group is highly non-abelian
