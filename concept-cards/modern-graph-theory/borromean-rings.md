# Quick Definition
The Borromean rings are three interlocking circles with the remarkable property that no two of them are linked, yet the three together cannot be separated.

# Core Definition
The Borromean rings are a three-component link shown in Fig. X.3 (p. 363). They are alternating. Despite being linked as a whole, any two components form a split (unlinked) link. The Jones polynomial confirms they are linked (Exercise 39).

# Prerequisites
- **Link** — The Borromean rings are a specific three-component link

# Key Properties
1. Three components
2. No two components are linked (pairwise unlinking)
3. The three components together are inseparable (linked)
4. Has an alternating diagram
