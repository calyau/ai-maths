# Quick Definition
Hadwiger's conjecture (1943): if χ(G) = k, then G is contractible to Kₖ. This deep conjecture contains the four-colour theorem as the case k = 5.

# Core Definition
**Hadwiger's conjecture** (Bollobás, p. 166): If χ(G) = k then G is contractible to Kₖ (i.e., Kₖ is a minor of G). The case k = 5 is equivalent to the four-colour theorem. The conjecture remains one of the most important open problems in graph theory for k ≥ 6.

# Prerequisites
- **Chromatic number** — the quantity in the hypothesis
- **Four colour theorem** — special case k = 5

# Key Properties
1. Proved for k ≤ 4 (easy), k = 5 (equivalent to 4CT), k = 6 (Robertson-Seymour-Thomas)
2. Open for k ≥ 7
3. Would imply the four-colour theorem
4. One of the deepest conjectures in graph theory
