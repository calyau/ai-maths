# Quick Definition
The figure-of-eight knot (knot $4_1$) has 4 crossings, is alternating, and is amphicheiral (equivalent to its mirror image). It contrasts with the trefoil, which is chiral.

# Core Definition
The figure-of-eight knot appears in Fig. VIII.4 and Fig. X.10 (p. 368). Exercise 34 (p. 381): "Show that the figure of eight knot is amphicheiral, that is it is ambient isotopic to its mirror image."

# Prerequisites
- **Knot** — The figure-of-eight is a specific knot

# Key Properties
1. Four crossings (denoted $4_1$ in Alexander-Briggs notation)
2. Alternating diagram
3. Amphicheiral: $L \sim L^*$ (equivalent to mirror image)
4. Contrasts with the trefoil, which is chiral
