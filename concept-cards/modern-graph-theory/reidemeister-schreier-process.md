# Quick Definition

The Reidemeister-Schreier process obtains a presentation of a subgroup $B$ from a presentation of $A$ and the Schreier diagram of $A$ mod $B$: generators are the chords, and relators are obtained by rewriting group relators at each vertex.

# Core Definition

**Theorem 3** (Bollobas, p. 264): Given $A = \langle a, b, \ldots \mid R_\mu, R_\nu, \ldots\rangle$ and the Schreier diagram of $A$ mod $B$, choose a spanning tree and label chords $c_j$. For each vertex $i$ and relator $R_\mu$, express the walk $R_\mu^i$ as a product of chord labels. The subgroup $B$ has presentation $(a_1, b_1, \ldots, a_2, b_2, \ldots \mid R_\mu^1, R_\nu^1, \ldots, R_\mu^2, R_\nu^2, \ldots)$.

# Prerequisites

- **Schreier diagram** -- The diagram from which the presentation is read
- **Group presentation** -- The input

# Key Properties

1. Generators of $B$ are the chords of a spanning tree of the Schreier diagram
2. The generators of $B$ are the decorations of the chords (Theorem 2)
3. Relators of $B$ come from evaluating each group relator at each vertex
4. The process is algorithmic and can be implemented on computers

# Construction / Recognition

## The Process
1. Construct the Schreier diagram of $A$ mod $B$
2. Choose a spanning tree; decorate $B$ with identity, tree edges with 1
3. The chord decorations generate $B$ (Theorem 2)
4. For each vertex $i$ and relator $R_\mu$: express the walk $R_\mu^i$ as a product of chord labels
5. These products are the relators of $B$

# Context & Application

The Reidemeister-Schreier process is fundamental in computational group theory. It transforms the abstract problem of finding a subgroup presentation into a concrete graph-theoretic procedure.

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Forgetting to evaluate relators at every vertex, not just vertex 1
  **Correction**: The relator $R_\mu^i$ must be computed for every vertex $i$

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: Thinking the process always gives a minimal presentation
  **Clarification**: The presentation may have redundant generators and relators; simplification is a separate step
