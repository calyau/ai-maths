# Competency Questions for "An Intuitionistic Theory of Types"

## Definitional (What is X?)
1. What is a type in Martin-Lof's intuitionistic theory?
2. What is a mathematical object (construction)?
3. What is a proposition in the propositions-as-types framework?
4. What is a proof in the propositions-as-types framework?
5. What is the Cartesian product (Pi type) of a family of types?
6. What is the disjoint union (Sigma type) of a family of types?
7. What is the function type A -> B?
8. What is the disjoint union A + B of two types?
9. What is the empty type (N_0)?
10. What is a universe (V) in type theory?
11. What is a small type vs. a large type?
12. What is a redex, and what is a normal form?
13. What is the Church-Rosser property?
14. What is a computable term (in the sense of the normalization proof)?

## Relational (How does X relate to Y?)
15. How does the Curry-Howard correspondence relate propositions to types?
16. How does universal quantification relate to the Pi type?
17. How does existential quantification relate to the Sigma type?
18. How does implication relate to the function type?
19. How does conjunction relate to the Cartesian product of two types?
20. How does disjunction relate to the disjoint union of two types?
21. How does the reflection principle relate to the universe V?
22. How does Girard's paradox relate to the assumption V in V?
23. How does first order predicate logic embed into the theory of types?
24. How does first order arithmetic embed into the theory of types?
25. How does the axiom of choice relate to the Sigma and Pi types?

## Procedural (How do I do X?)
26. How do I construct a function (lambda abstraction)?
27. How do I apply a function (Pi elimination)?
28. How do I form a pair (Sigma introduction)?
29. How do I eliminate a pair (Sigma elimination / projections)?
30. How do I define a function by cases on A + B?
31. How do I define a function by recursion on natural numbers?
32. How do I translate a first order derivation into a term of the theory of types?
33. How do I normalize a term?

## Prerequisite (What before X?)
34. What must I know before understanding dependent types?
35. What must I know before understanding the normalization theorem?
36. What must I know before understanding Girard's paradox?
37. What must I know before understanding the translation of arithmetic?

## Diagnostic (What distinguishes X from Y?)
38. What distinguishes introduction rules from elimination rules?
39. What distinguishes contraction from reduction from conversion?
40. What distinguishes the Pi type from the Sigma type?
41. What distinguishes the function type A -> B from the Pi type?
42. What distinguishes small types from large types?
43. What distinguishes the intuitionistic axiom of choice from its classical counterpart?
44. What distinguishes common errors with type membership from correct usage?
45. What distinguishes the type N_0 (falsehood) from the type N_1 (truth)?
