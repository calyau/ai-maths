# Competency Questions for Groups and Symmetry (Armstrong, 1988)

## Definitional (What is X?)
1. What is a group?
2. What is a subgroup?
3. What is a permutation?
4. What is an isomorphism between groups?
5. What is a homomorphism?
6. What is a normal subgroup?
7. What is a quotient group?
8. What is a group action?
9. What is a cyclic group?
10. What is a dihedral group?
11. What is the symmetric group?
12. What is the alternating group?
13. What is a coset?
14. What is a conjugacy class?
15. What is a free group?

## Relational (How does X relate to Y?)
1. How do subgroups relate to the parent group's order (Lagrange's theorem)?
2. How does the kernel of a homomorphism relate to normal subgroups?
3. How do conjugacy classes partition a group?
4. How do orbits and stabilizers relate to each other (Orbit-Stabilizer theorem)?
5. How do the symmetric and alternating groups relate?
6. How do direct products relate to the constituent groups?
7. How does the point group of a wallpaper group relate to its translation subgroup?
8. How does the commutator subgroup relate to abelianisation?
9. How do Sylow subgroups relate to the prime factorization of |G|?
10. How do lattices and point groups constrain wallpaper patterns?

## Procedural (How do I do X?)
1. How do I determine the symmetry group of a solid?
2. How do I compute a multiplication table for a dihedral group?
3. How do I decompose a permutation into disjoint cycles?
4. How do I determine whether two groups are isomorphic?
5. How do I find all subgroups of a finite group?
6. How do I compute conjugacy classes?
7. How do I classify finitely generated abelian groups using row/column operations?
8. How do I count orbits using the Counting Theorem (Burnside's lemma)?
9. How do I determine the wallpaper group of a pattern?
10. How do I construct a semidirect product?

## Prerequisite (What before X?)
1. What must I know before understanding group actions?
2. What must I know before understanding quotient groups?
3. What must I know before understanding the Sylow theorems?
4. What must I know before understanding free groups and presentations?
5. What must I know before understanding wallpaper patterns?
6. What must I know before understanding the classification of finitely generated abelian groups?

## Diagnostic (What distinguishes X from Y?)
1. What distinguishes a homomorphism from an isomorphism?
2. What distinguishes a normal subgroup from an ordinary subgroup?
3. What distinguishes even and odd permutations?
4. What distinguishes a direct product from a semidirect product?
5. What distinguishes the five types of lattices?
6. What distinguishes common errors from common confusions in applying Lagrange's theorem?
7. What distinguishes a free group from a finitely presented group?
8. What distinguishes a group action from a group acting freely?
